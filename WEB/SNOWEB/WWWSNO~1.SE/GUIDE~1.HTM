What type of skier/snowboarder are you?

Are you a extreme hot-shot, crazy snowboarder, a partyanimal, a soft glider, a ski bum or a family man? Try SnoWeb's personal guide of which ski resort will give you the most.

Note that this information is personal and covers only a few of the Swedish ski resorts. For more information about the resorts go to the complete list.
Extreme: You think skiing in the prepared piste is just a transportation to a mogulcourse or a steep offpist run, unless you have a chickenrace with your friends straight down. You always search new tough challenges such as big cliff jumps, steep corridors or high mountainpeaks to climb.

Yes, I fit the description!

Party: You enjoy a short day of skiing, but you always try to find a bar in the slopes around lunchtime. Then the only skiing you do after is down to the Apres Ski for more drinks. The next day you barely make it up to the slopes in time for a lunchbeer.

Yes, I fit the description!

Jibber:You enjoy snowboarding in big baggy clothes and you try to spend as much time off the ground as on it. You hang around the snowboardpark with your bluehaired, pierced friends and afterwards you enjoy busting it to Rage Against the Machine in the disco.

Yes, I fit the description!

Glider:
You enjoy skiing, but only if you can cruise down an easy piste. Your outfit and equipment are as important as your style in the slopes. The social life with your friends is what makes your vacation worthwhile.

Yes, I fit the description!

Svensson:
You look for a quiet cheap place for the family that offer nice and easy skiing for the kids. You like taking your wife on some distant crosscountry trail, while your kids are in skischool. You enjoy living in a small cabin or an apartment where you can do your own cooking.

Yes, I fit the description!

Skibum:
You think hotels and restaurants are an unnecessary expence and you just want to ski as cheaply as possible. Why just stay one week when you can stay for months? The slopes should be tough and the beer cheap on a perfect resort and you do not mind sharing room with 10 smelly skibums, with duct-tape on their skiing equipment.

Yes, I fit the description!
