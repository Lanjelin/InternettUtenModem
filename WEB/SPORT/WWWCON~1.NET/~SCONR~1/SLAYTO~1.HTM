Nancy A. Slayton Award

for Meritorious Service to the NATC

First given in 1984, the Nancy A. Slayton Award was formulated and is sponsored by Pena Taurina Sol y Sombra of San Francisco, California, to honor exceptional service to the National Association of Taurine Clubs of the United States of America. The criteria for this Award are as follows:

  1. 1. The recipient of this Award:
    • a. Shall have been a member, for a period of at least five (5) years, of any pena participating in the NATC; and
    • b. Shall, during that period, have been actively involved in the NATC, with service as an officer, board member, or committee member for at least two (2) of those years; and
    • c. Shall have attended and participated in at least four (4) Congresos of the NATC and contributed in some meaningful way to the NATC.
  2. 2. The recipient of this Award must be a citizen or a permanent resident of the United States of America.
  3. 3. This Award may not be given to the same recipient more than once.
  4. 4. This Award may be made posthumously.
  5. 5. This need not be an annual Award.
  6. 6. Nominations for the Award shall be made from the floor at the first business meeting of each Congreso.
  7. 7. Only delegates may vote on this Award. The vote shall be by secret ballot. A simple majority is required. In case of a tie vote, the President of the NATC shall cast the deciding vote.
  8. 8. This Award shall be dated the following calendar year, so that the recipient may be present to accept the Award at the following Congreso.

(As amended at the 29th Congreso of the NATC, Aguascalientes, Ags., Mexico, April 25, 1991)
