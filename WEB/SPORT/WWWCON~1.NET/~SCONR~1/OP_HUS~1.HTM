O. P. Huston Award

for Conspicuous Achievement
in the World of Tauromaquia

First awarded in 1968, the O.P. Houston Award is to be conferred according to the rules as set forth below and is made to honor the recipients of this Award and as a loving tribute to the memory of a grand aficionado and friend, Col. O.P. Houston.

The criteria for the O.P. Houston Award as established by the 1968 convention of the National Association of Taurine Clubs of the United States of America, as amended by this and other conventions and congresos, are as follows:

  1. 1. This Award is given to a person who, during the lifetime of the NATC, has contributed exemplary service to the Taurine World, and has conveyed to others an enlightened view of the history and art of La Fiesta Brava.
  2. 2. The recipient of this Award must be a citizen or a permanent resident of the United States of America.
  3. 3. This Award may not be given to the same recipient more than once.
  4. 4. This Award may be made posthumously.
  5. 5. This need not be an annual Award.
  6. 6. Nominations for the Award shall be made from the floor at the first business meeting of each congreso or convention.
  7. 7. Only delegates may vote on this Award. The vote shall be by secret ballot. A simple majority is required. In case of a tie vote, the President of the NATC shall cast the deciding vote.
  8. 8. This Award shall be dated the following calendar year, so that the recipient may be present to accept the Award at the following congreso or convention.

(As amended at the 29th Congreso of the NATC, Aguascalientes, Ags., Mexico, April 25, 1991)
