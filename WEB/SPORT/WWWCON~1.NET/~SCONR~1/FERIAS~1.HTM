Ferias of Taurine Interest

Las Fallas Annual celebration in Valencia, Spain -- always ending on March 19, the eve of the Feast of St. Joseph

Feria de Abril Annual celebration in Sevilla, Spain.

Feri de Cali Annual celebration in Cali, Colombia -- always, Dec. 25 to Jan. 3. (en español -- '94 feria info, some of general applicability to each year's festivities). Info also available from
Latino Net's Cali-information page.

Feria de Córdoba Annual celebration in Córdoba, Spain (in español).

Feria de Gijón -- An essay discussing the taurine activities during the '95 feria on the north coast of Spain (Asturias)

Feria Internacional del Caballo, Texcoco -- 19th annual feria, 1996 (March 21 to April 14).

Feria de León -- Annual celebration in León, Guanajuato, México -- January 10 to February 9, 1997.

Feria de Málaga (en español) Annual celebration in Málaga, Spain.

La Féria de Nîmes Annual celebration in Nîmes, France.

La Féria de San Fermín Annual romp in Pamplona -- this link is to a site that is, basically, an advertisement for an on-line service. But, recognizing that, it IS the best collection of Pamplona links we've found.

Feria de San Marcos Annual celebration in Aguascalientes, México ('96 corrida schedule, plus information of general applicability to each year's festivities) (also called La Feria de México); another page featuring a description of the feria (en español) maintained, in a long listing of Mexican ferias and expositions, on the large "México 2000 Infoserver";

Feria del Señor de los Milagros Annual celebration (50th Anniversary in '96) in Lima, Peru -- with a series of bullfights in Plaza de Acho. Dates vary, but begins the final week of October, and runs through the end of November.

Museums

The Bullfighting Museum (Museu Taurí in Catalan), Barcelona

The Bullfighting Museum in Bilbao (at the Vista Allegre Plaza)

Museo Taurino, Cordoba -- photo I, photo II(short description of collection in Spanish)

Museo Taurino, Huamantla, Tlaxcala, México

Museo Taurino, Rhonda -- at the Rhonda bullring (one of the loveliest and important --at least historically -- in Spain). A fine collection of memorabilia collected and displayed thanks to the efforts of a group of major Spanish taurine figures (including the American ex-matador-turned-artist, John Fulton)

Travel Miscellany

  • Bullfights in Cancun -- well, not exactly. Want to know how much reliance you can place on interent search engines (sure you'd have a hard time surfing without them, but ...)? If you search with "Lycos" using the term "bullfight," the second result returned is this site (with a 99% "relevancy" rating) -- but, it's nothing but a VERY conventional bullfight poster and the words "Olé - Bullfights and Mexican Tradition." That's it ... no more. A little disappointing from a site intended to 'promote' Mexico "and it's many riches." That's why we're trying to annotate Mundo Taurino [Internet]'s links for you.
  • Bullfights in Cartagena -- (en español) -- brief mention of the January bullfight season in Cartagena, Colombia (including hotel and tourism info. addresses)
  • Bullfights in Korea -- (in english) -- yes, no typo here. Bullfights (of a sort) are part of the annual Kaech'on Art Festival in Chinju, South Korea (about 95 km west of Pusan)
  • Caracas, Venezuela -- brief mention of bullfighting at Nuevo Circo, "one of the four most important bullrings in Latin America" -- and of a Venezuelan taurine event called "Toros Coleados"-- in the "Venezuela for Kids" pages maintained by the Venezuelan Embassy in Washington DC.
  • Fiesta, Feuerwerk, Flamenco (auf Deutsch) A travel feature story from "Wochenpost Online," discussing taurine travel possibilities during August and September, in and around Madrid. (e-mail MT[I] for English translation, courtesy of Marcus Hermann)
  • Run with the Bulls: The Adventurer's Guide to The Sun Also Rises
    by Craig Boreth. If you've a desire to " Drink your way through Paris and party in Pamplona at the Fiesta de San Fermin -- Dine on Tapas in San Sebastian and dance all night in Madrid -- Hike, fish and swim in the Spanish Pyrenees -- Learn to RUN WITH THE BULLS safely, and watch a bullfight dangerously" ... well, then this might just be the book for you! Nice picture of the encierro too ...
  • "Softguide Madrid" -- online tour guide to Madrid, including a page of information (plans to provide much more) about bullfighting in general, and prices/schedules at Las Ventas.
  • Spain: Men in Tights -- brief references to things taurine in the website maintained by the VERY high-quality travel-info publishers, Lonely Planet (includes a lovely photo of a traje de luces -- gold on white).
  • Travelspain: Custom Tours
    This agency (in Caceres, we think) says they can create an individualized taurine adventure for you. Just tell them what you'd like to do -- from getting drunk in Pamplona to organizing your own bullfight (!) -- and, they'll take care of it. Anyone care to put them to the test, and then get back to MT[I] with the results?
