Bibliotheca Kierkegaardiana

Bibliotheca Kierkegaardiana is a series of books in 16 volumes on major Kierkegaardian topics. The series is published in the major languages, chiefly English, written by an international group of experts and Kierkegaard scholars.

Volume 1
Kierkegaard's View of Christianity

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen
ISBN: 87 7421 0041


The contributors to this volume provide an objective presentation of the historical background and development of Kierkegaard's view of Christianity. Each author has drawn his own conclusions, and though their views may not always be in harmony, the editors have naturally not tried to unify them.

Volume 2
The Sources and Depths of Faith in Kierkegaard

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen
ISBN: 87 7421 0068


Kierkegaard's view of the Bible is treated from the standpoint of "revelation", his assesment of prayer and the life of prayer, his view of Christianity, from the perspective of the absurd, seriousness, inwardness.
Reference is made to Kierkegaard's views on Christian virtues, love, and mercy, and from the perspective of corrective is presented Kierkegaard's understanding of an apostle's role and the Christian who must deny himself in order to die from the world. - Did Kierkegaard wish to reintroduce asceticism? This volume deals with the problem together with the subject of death.

Volume 3
Concepts and Alternatives in Kierkegaard

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1980
ISBN: 87-7421-244-3


Kierkegaard's world of thought was affected by the philosophical streams and discussion of his time. He actively took part in the critique of philosophical systems as well as thinkers of his century. He evaluated them in his own way, both psychologically and philosophically, confronting them with older disciplines, i.e. Aristotelian logic, and with the values of Christianity.
This volume offers both longer and shorter articles, written by recognized Kierkegaard specialists, all presenting an analysis of a special concept or a distinctive problem, important for understanding the work of the Danish philosopher.

Volume 4
Kierkegaard and Speculative Idealism

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1979
ISBN: 87-7421-211-7


This volume shows Kierkegaard's opposition to philosophical systems which reduced man to an insignificant non-entity and, with premature optimism, understood world historical development as a progress towards Utopia, an imagined paradise on earth.
History has confirmed the accuracy of Kierkegaard's criticism of speculative philosophy, a criticism which, hitting also at totalitarian political systems, thereby demonstrates his relevance for our time.

Volume 5
Theological Concepts in Kierkegaard.

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1980
ISBN: 87-7421-269-9


The articles in this volume are focused upon Kierkegaard's theological activity: his responses to theological issues which arose in the Church and University of his time. As a Lutheran Church, it also inherited the legacy of an older confessional tradition. In his theological writings, Kierkegaard confronted both this Lutheran theological tradition as well as the theological responses of the early nineteenth century Danish Church, often producing his own theological formulations.

Volume 6
Kierkegaard and Great Traditions

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1981
ISBN: 87-7421-291-5


Kierkegaard was neither a historian of philosophy nor a historian of theology. He did not read Plato or Aristotle, Augustine or Luther (to mention only the greatest names) for their sakes but for his own sake, in the service of his own purposes.
Kierkegaard was an extremely critical reader who kept his distance more often than he gave his endorsement. The first response and also the second are found in the articles in this volume.

Volume 7
Kierkegaard and Human Values

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1980
ISBN: 87-7421-281-8


Kierkegaard was a rebellious child of his time. Living in the culture of the nineteenth century, with its own particular political, philosophical and social outlook, he related to it in his own way. He dealth with its aesthetic and ethical-religious values in theory and experiment, handling such themes as human responsibility and egoism, love despair, loneliness, and crime. All these topics and many more are fully treated in this volume.

Volume 8
The Legacy and Interpretation of Kierkegaard

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1981
ISBN: 87-7421-315-6


The theme of this volume is one of the must difficult, because it requires from the authors of the present studies a summit of erudition and understanding of their topics.
The editors of this series succeeded in gathering such specialists. The reader can find here the results of their penetrating studies of the influence and interpretation of Kierkegaard in America, England, France, Germany, Norway, and Sweden, in addition with separate studies of Kierkegaard in relation to Sartre, Karl Barth, Bultmann, and others.

Volume 9
Kierkegaard Literary Miscellany

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1981
ISBN: 87-7421-308-3


Belles-lettres was extremely important for Søren Kierkegaard. He was inspired by it, and it offered him images and figures, problems and solutions of life, playing the role of a life-experience, as good literature always does.
But Kierkegaard was a writer himself, choosing his own style, creating his own command of language.
About the must important literary influences - namely the Danish ones, and about other ones, the reader can find some examples in this volume.

Volume 10
Kierkegaard's Teachers

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1982
ISBN: 87-7421-340-7


The articles in this volume present some of the teachers in Kierkegaard's life. These he met at school, at church and in the university, while he became acquainted with others by reading their books and letters.
Only few of these teachers had any deep or lasting influence on Kierkegaard. Just as he did not want to have disciples, he was not a disciple of any teacher himself.
As a son of his age, Kierkegaard knew his teacher's way of thinking and expression of problems, but he made his own original formulations and answers.
Everything made an impression on his sensitive genius, but no teacher could call him his disciple.
Two philosophers in different ways significant for Kierkegaard are dealt with in other volumes in this series: Hegel (vol. 4) and Socrates (vol. 14).

Volume 11
The Copenhagen of Kierkegaard

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1986
ISBN: 87-7421-526-4


Søren Kierkegaard was born, lived andd died in Copenhagen. He loved the city, the streets, its buildings, its people, and its cultural life.
In spite of the difficulties he experienced with the people of Copenhagen, he could not have parted with them.
This volume is an historical guide to Kierkegaard's Copenhagen. It takes the reader through the streets and marketplaces, witnessing the scenes and the life of the city that were so well-known to Kierkegaard, himself. It introduces the reader to the writers who helped to create the cultural climate in "the King's Copenhagen" of the 19th century.
See also vol. 10, 12 and 13 in this series.

Volume 12
Kierkegaard as a Person

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1983
ISBN: 87-7421-382-2


The personal aspect of Kierkegaard's life forms the background to his thought and view of existence. His attitudes and battles (especially those with the Press), were influenced by a combination of factors: his family, his one and only love, his best friend, also his school, his university and his choice of books.
The influence of all these is to be found reflected not only in his work as an author, but also in his private prayers as they are known to us and in his entire attitude as a Christian which, as with everything else about him, was also dialectical. Even his death is not without an element of mystery.

Volume 13
Kierkegaard and the Church in Denmark

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1984
ISBN: 87-7421-432-2


Anyone who has ever read Kierkegaard or merely knows him by reputation is aware that he employed the full extent of his powers in an unrelenting struggle with "Christendom" which in his own time was especially represented to him by the Danish Church. Far fewer would have any concept of what this Church was like as an institution, which theological theories predominated in its teaching,and how it generally functioned. It will scarely be possible to present even an approximate understanding and evaluation of Kierkegaard's own interpretation and evaluation without knowledge of the actual conditions of the time.

Volume 14
Kierkegaard's Classical Inspiration

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1985
ISBN: 87-7421-446-2


Classical literature was close to Kierkegaard's heart. Not only his contemporaries, his school, but also his teachers, taught him to appreciate antiquity. Figures such as Antigone in mythology, and particularly Socrates, in the platonic or the aristophanic form, played a central part in his thought, and of course Plato himself.
On Plato and Aristotle, see also vol. 6 and 10 in this series.

Volume 15
Kierkegaard Research

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1987
ISBN: 87-7421-566-3


Translated into many languages, the work of Søren Kierkegaard has become the property of the world. The rich variety of his thought and the nature of his life has meant that as a philosopher, theologian, man-of-letters, as well as the subject of psychological and other studies, the Danish thinker has been the object of research in many and various cultural circles and countries.
This volume affords a survey of main areas of Kierkegaard research, which has been conducted in the U.S.A. and parts of Europe, including Scandinavia.

Volume 16
Some of Kierkegaard's Main Categories

Editors: Niels Thulstrup and Marie Milulavá Thulstrup
Publisher: C.A. Reitzels Boghandel A/S, Copenhagen, 1988
ISBN: 87-7421-575-2


This volume includes studies analyzing some of the main categories of Søren Kierkegaard's thought: the single individual, the notions of freedom, reason, passion, trial, edification, subjectivity and truth, and his division of life into different stages.
Kierkegaard transformed and evaluated the philosophical and theological conceptions of his age in an original manner, which is characteristic and typical of his work and thought.
For other main concepts in Kierkegaard, see volumes 2, 3, 4, and 10 of this series.
