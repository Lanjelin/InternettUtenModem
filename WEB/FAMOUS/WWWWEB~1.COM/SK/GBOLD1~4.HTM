(Dedicated to the City of Kotka, Finland)

Dreams of KOTKA


Positioned like clouds having no air
To sway them over eager lands,
Bereft of diligent brilliance from above
And there
Hands open
Placed in unpopulated deserts,
Comes
By way of saturation
A dandy propensity
To have the naught,
Shamed by braveless densities,
So then to bestow
By soiled grace
Upon cities spotted with dim lights
My ethereal execution,
Like waves with no ocean
To feel the nipple of every crest.

So
With dreams of KOTKA
I sail aimlessly
By will
To await to be revitalized
By a stoic nordic awakening.

--Anthony L. Pecoraro, 6-27-96 (Book 36) c.1996
