The Subject: re: an exercise in futility
At 22:36:27 on 09/23/96, schmitt (schmitt@uclink4.berkeley.edu) wrote:

As applicable to Fear and Trembling:
In the Preamble to the Heart isn't god really just a circumstance? Isn't the point of describing the story of Abraham to depict a knight of faith whose defining relationship is to his son? God asked him to sacrifice what was most beloved by Abraham and it was Isaac. Abraham as a knight of faith, was so confident in the fact that he would receive Isaac again that he could kill him. To fully understand the idea of the knight of faith one has to view god's command as a circumstance of life. It is comparable to a life threatening
disease. The reason for the re-telling of the story in Problema I & II, as far as I see it,(having little to do with faith or resignation)was to describe the suspension of the ethical- The idea that what is ethical is pliable to the circumstance. Kierkegaard was a confused homosexual, I think trying to justify his feelings. (Not that homosexuality has anything to do with morals.) The real question that this poses, in my opinion, is "can there really be a suspension of the ethical? Is there no truth or is it really as adolescent as it sounds?" The existentialist will answer that question one way, the objectivist another. . . go ahead answer it. Please.
