All I have to offer in my defense for the dangling dependent clause in the original is that I get telegraphic when I'm going over concepts that I think people will accept as already settled. Your parodied exegesis of how confusion relates to consciousness and the border between object and subject is frighteningly convincing (although the "lard" references are more condescending than I deserve). To avoid being tarred as the progenitor of such monstrosities, I hereby disavow and recant a belief (1) in the existence of some sort of short-lived, naif "experience" of the objective that is prior to or purer than subjective "interaction" with the world or (2) that "confusion" is the inevitable result of human interaction with the world. (And yet, I am heard to mutter, there is confusion, and it does seem inevitable.)
