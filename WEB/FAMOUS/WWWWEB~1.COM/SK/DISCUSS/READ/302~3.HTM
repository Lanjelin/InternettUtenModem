At 13:32:16 on 01/07/97, Tom Dean (tsd@ogk.com) wrote:

At 12:53:55 PST on 01-06-97, L. Rempe wrote:

>I need two pieces of information. Where did Kierkegaard stand on the subject of the Holy Trinity? On the Abraham/Isaac sacrifice story? Is there a good ten cent explanation? If not, can you direct me to any of his writings?

For the story of Abraham's sacrifice of Isaac, see the opening section of _Fear and Trembling_. SK uses the story to define and illustrate a particular virtue, a unique response to the demands of faith. SK's focus shifts back and forth from Abraham to other characters, but Abrahahm stays central throughout the first 60 pages or so. To the extent there is a ten cent explanation of his views, SK gives it himself in the "Preliminary Expectoration".

If this is your first time dipping into SK, do not be put off by the Victorian stylistic flourishes, and be aware that SK is criticizing Hegelianism, among other things.
