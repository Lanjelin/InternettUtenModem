Kierkegaard is saying that sin is really an impossibility because all philosophy begins in doubt. For doubt to be at all a certain certainty was prior to the doubting. "Sin" is therefore either a reality in despair (over its non-being which only seems like being), or an illusion of despair which negates the appearance of Being (a priori).
