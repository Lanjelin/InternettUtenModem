The Subject: Re: 'Offense' and the Virgin Birth
At 07:34:50 on 02/05/97, David Tye (tye@datacube.com) wrote:

At 22:10:54 PST on 02-04-97, Cortese wrote:

>Without having to go as far as adultry, and keeping the doctrine of original sin in mind, God's incarnation of human form in Jesus is a quite literal example of God coming into the world through sin.
>Mr. Tye, does this mean our diety possesses an ends justifies the means philosophy?
>

I am not sure to what doctrine of Original Sin you are referring. The traditional and orthodox doctrine holds that Jesus, as the unspotted Lamb of God, was not subject to Original Sin. Roman Catholic belief holds that Mary was not subject to it either.

In any case, I don't see the relevance of Original Sin to Mr. Dean's point. He was hinting that the offense occasioned by Mary's low social standing would be intensified if her social standing were even lower due to some sort of scandal. If the scandal is merely that she was subject to Original Sin, I don't see how this could affect her social standing since everyone else was subject to it as well. I think he was hinting at some sort of scandalous sin such as adultery.
