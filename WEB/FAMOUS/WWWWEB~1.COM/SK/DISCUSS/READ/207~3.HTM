>>At 16:54:15 PST on 11-15-96, Tom Dean wrote:
>Am I the only one who has no idea what is meant by the phrase "the Absolute"? Can anyone provide a jargon-free definition of the Absolute? I am of the opinion that terms like "the Absolute" (capital "A") are prime instances of the bewitchment of our intelligenc by means of language. It seems that when we use the term "absolute," we usually do so in a particular context. Someone asks me if I am sure my pseudonym is J. Climacus. My reply: Absolutely. My point is that terms such "the absolute" was given meaning in a concrete context. When we remove it from that context, we get the feeling that we're mouthing some profound mystical insight, when we are, in fact, saying something that can have no meaning because it is unmoored from the concrete context (language game) that gave it meaning in the first place. It is as if our lips are moving,but no sound coming out. It is Wittgenstein's gear box in which the gears spin in resolute isolation; they appear to be accomplishing something, but they do no work.

The individual whois infinately intersted inhis own eternal happiness can get asmuch use out of Wittgenstein's tool box as he can get outof history.
