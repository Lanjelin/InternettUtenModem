The Subject: Re: Abraham
At 11:24:51 on 08/26/96, J. Climacus (brettdel@mail.aros.net) wrote:

Well, sure: My child is brain-dead, in a vegetative state, and
god says to take him out. I'll buy that (or at least that's a possible
situation in which I might consider it). Or maybe god tells me my son
is going to grow up to be the next Hitler. I might go along with that as
well. But I think the point of the Abraham parable is that Abraham was
expected to do it just because god sez so. Any reasons god might give
to JUSTIFY his commands means he is in some way subservient to a code
of ethics or morality, that he is not the source of what is right, but
is constrained by convention or tradition or human morality to only do
that which is acceptable to mere mortals. I'm perfectly happy to accept
this conception of god (although I don't think such a god would be any
more likely to exist). But that's not the god of Abraham or the god of
orthodox christianity.
