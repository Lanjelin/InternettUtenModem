>Yes, faith is the essential thing. Lack of faith is simply renunciation of God. With faith we struggle to get close to him, to approximate true understanding as well as mortals can; without it we posit ourselves as God, and the miseries that lie down that path are numinous and never-ending. As a former atheist I can testify to that. We may not gain everything through faith, but we gain the essential things. The question is, why do we, as Christians, have the essential things and still remain lost, despairing, crazed with grief over this curse of a life that was meant by our loving Creator as a gift?
>Sid Scott

To begin with, why should ³faith² have to be understood in a religious sense? What of faith in Man? Faith in goodness? Faith in truth?
Faith must not be considered as ³blind obedience,² but rather trust. Trust in any relationship is relying upon the designated other, but it is never
done blindly, but rather with the understanding of each others¹ strengths and weaknesses.
Faith in this respect can ultimately lead to greater strength, resolve and determination, making the faithful man the warrior
in a sometimes harsh world.
