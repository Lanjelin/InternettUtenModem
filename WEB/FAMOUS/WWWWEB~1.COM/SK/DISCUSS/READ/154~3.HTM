The Subject: Re: melancholy and despair
At 16:39:51 on 11/01/96, T. Dean (tsd@ogk.com) wrote:

At 12:58:50 PST on 10-29-96, jeff mason wrote:

>Can anyone tell me about the difference between melancholy an despair in K's writings. Does despair have a phenomenological content, or is it only a structural feature of people without Christian faith.
>
>Thanks

I'm not sure what SK thinks melancholy is, but I think it is distinct from despair. SK posits despair as the result of an insufficient response to the absolute's demands of the single individual (I am using the terms as translated in the Hvongs' edition of Fear and Trembling and Sickness Unto Death). Despair is thus tied to a failure to respond to the call of the divine -- that is, sin. We awake from despair into an awareness of despair, which negatives (or at least reduces) our desperation.

Melancholy is distinct from despair in that the essence of despair is our unconsciousness of it, and melancholy is an emotion we are at least aware of feeling. My question is whether melancholy is linked to despair -- is it an intermediate feeling between despair and grace?
