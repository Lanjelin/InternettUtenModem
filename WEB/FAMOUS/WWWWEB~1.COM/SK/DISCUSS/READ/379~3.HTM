At 13:10:43 on 02/16/97, Frank Lynch (frankl@interport.net) wrote:

The details of Jesus' baptism can be found in Matt 3:13-16, Mark 1:9-11, Luke 3:21-22. John does not have Jesus being explicitly baptised by JBap, but does have JBap singling Jesus out (1:32-36). (If you don't have a New Testament, email me, and I'll email you the relevant texts.)

The reasons for Jesus' baptism have been a matter of great debate. In "A Marginal Jew," John Meier (who in this work is attempting to deliver an unbiased set of conclusions) concludes that there is little in the stories of Jesus which are more likely to be true than the story of Jesus being baptised by JBap. (I remind you, he is trying to write from an unbiased perspective. Safe bet that, as a man of the cloth, his own feelings are different.) His reasons for this conclusion are that the event is of considerable potential embarassment to the early church, and that it is in three of the gospels, rather than being left behind. Too well known to be excluded...

Now, the RC Catechism (paragraph 438) associates the baptism with a revelation of his messianic consecration, tied to "his divind mission". Others have seen the baptism as an expression of unity between Jesus and his people, many of whom were also being baptised.

Parenthetically, I want to point out (NOW HEAR THIS!) that when I suggested the idea that Jesus may have been raised above Original Sin at his baptism, I was quickly responding to your question about who might have raised him to this level... I myself don't think his incarnation waited until then, but only meant ot raise a possibility. I hope I did no harm with this suggestion.
