The Subject: Re: faithful vs. unfaithful man
At 14:57:41 on 08/30/96, Anthony L. Pecoraro (alp@loop.com) wrote:

At 14:29:02 PST on 08-27-96, rock wrote:

>I have to live by faith, by what K. calls 'dependence'. It is a moment to moment choice I make. I chose to see myself as utterly dependent. I realize that I could never breathe my breath or make my heart beat with any regularity by my own force of will. That both humbles me and pisses me off. I try to make a practice of not being unfaithful to what I realize is my true condition. I try to cheer up in spite of my utter finitude; to make a game of my lack of ultimate control. Is it wonderful to be alive? I chose 'yes'. Faith is giving substance to something (or someone) which is -as yet-UNSEEN.

Then do we exist by self-choice or deliberate design of
another dominion? You certainly have the choice life's
desinence--even a certain freedom of movement within
laws of this domain.

Certainly we are dependant on the Fiery Father of this
singular system, yet still does He imbue a rather
novel independence.

You may have faith in this or not.

It is not about control, but mastery.

(Again we note the conceptual (cognitive) ambiguity
-- the on going mindful paradox -- of absolute and
relative Truth, and that which is transcendent to thought.)


ALP, 8/30/96 [1] (Book 37)
