I see the significance as such (and I may be completely wrong):
I see the point of the story (SK's presentation) to demonstrate that there are incomprehensible contradictions, frustrations, and nuances in life. If one never had faith in something extra, something over and above this reality, one would be resigned to despair; as in the man who resigns that he will never again regain the princess (F&T). Instead we must have faith; as the knight of faith believes he will get the girl back, on the strength of the absurd. We must have faith in the absurd, in order to coninue life.
This theme continues in Sickness Unto Death. If we don't take a spiritual risk, we get no spiritual profit. (Similarly, in F&T, "he who works takes home the bread"; Or maybe even Socrates, "the unexamined life is not worth living"). If we don't do this we are simply mindless. The true knight of faith, is silent and does not try to teach, because he will not be understood. The journey to become a true christian is one you must make by yourself. If we don't do this, we will be in despair- for what reason for existance, or purpose can we evidence for ourselves?
This is how I see where SK is going on a more macro level.
