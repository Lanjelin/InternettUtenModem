The Subject: Re: The role of history in faith.
At 14:50:55 on 11/19/96, J. Climacus (brettdel@mail.aros.net) wrote:


>The individual whois infinately intersted inhis own eternal happiness can get asmuch use out of Wittgenstein's tool box as he can get outof history.

I think I said "gear box" . . . but, no matter.

I believe in happiness, as in life, liberty, etc. But is it OK to sacrifice truth in exchange for happiness? There are all sorts of happy thoughts -- everything from Hobbits to UFOs to ESP -- that have little basis in fact. And yet believing in these things seems to add a poetry to life that is otherwise lacking. But maybe that's enough. We imagine Don Quixote to be happy in his obliviousness to the real world. As a purely personal preference, I find it hard to foment credulity for the sake of happiness. If there is a God, I believe he would like us to use our god-given powers of understanding rather than simply capitulate to a belief system merely because it is most apt to produce happiness.
