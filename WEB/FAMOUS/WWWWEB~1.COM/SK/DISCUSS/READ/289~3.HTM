At 16:17:35 on 12/31/96, Martin (m.waldenvik@swipnet.se) wrote:

At 11:48:05 PST on 12-03-96, J. Climacus wrote:

>Is it possible to love selflessly? SK seems to think so, but it's hard for me to see this as anything but a pipe dream. Certainly, there are works of love that are more or less selfless, but is it really possible to love everyone indiscriminately? Is it even desireable? Is this what Christianity requires, or is SK simply transmogrifying the message?

I thought that your question is very imported. Christianity is about love, thats right. Jesus says in Matthew 22:37. "..."Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your mind. This is the first and greatest commandment. And the second is like it: ´Love your neighbour as yourself. All the Law and the Prophets hang on these two commandments."

Love your neighbour as yourself. You can never love another human being without loving yoursef. This is not selfish love. They do not split, You can only love yourself if you love your brother, and you can only love your brother if you love yourself. But whats so important is that you cannot love yourself and not your brother int the right way if you not is filled with the love of Christ. I´m not telling that the one that not are christian not have the love, because love is in the men. But the true love of Christ is something different. And to many of the christians does not love in the right way, like Jesus teached.

And we need to understand the love that the Lord gave us when he let his own Son die on the cross for our sins. When we understand that love and believe, then can we reach the true love. And love our self and our neighbour and God, and become real human.

God loves the men, He became a man because of his love to us. I think this is the most important message to humans today. God became a man for YOU! He died for YOU, and rose from the dead for YOUR rescue. He loves you more then YOU ever can understand. To understand is not imported here, the important thing is our belief in God and his Word.
