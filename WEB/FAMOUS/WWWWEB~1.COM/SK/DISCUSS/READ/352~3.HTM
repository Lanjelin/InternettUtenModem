The Subject: Re: 'Offense' and the Virgin Birth
At 05:27:37 on 02/07/97, David Tye (tye@datacube.com) wrote:

At 17:16:41 PST on 02-06-97, Cortese wrote:

>Therefore it is not "offensive", the way SK means it, if we were to discover definitively that Mary had committed adultry. And it is more "offensive" to learn of the Immaculate Conception.
>But if we were using "offense" in the traditional manner, our reactions would be reversed.
>Is this correct?
>Have I understood?
>

I think you mean Virgin Birth rather than Immaculate Conception. The doctrine of the Immaculate Conception relates to whether or not Mary was born with the stain of Original Sin. It has nothing to do with the conception or birth of Jesus.

The answer to your question is, I believe, yes. The idea that Mary conceived Jesus through adultery is not at all offensive to worldly understanding. In fact, it is the skeptic's preferred view of things. Offense occurs when the divine understanding collides with mere human understanding. According to the merely human understanding, it is only possible for women to conceive through sex. So the merely human understanding must hold that Mary conceived via sex one way or another. Since she wasn't married at the time, she must be guilty of adultery. The worldly understanding is quite happy with this view of things. People commit adultery all the time.

But does a woman become pregnant by the power of the Holy Spirit? The divine understanding (at least as posited by the New Testament) says the answer is yes. This answer is unacceptable to the merely human understanding, and so the merely human understanding takes offense. It rejects the Virgin Birth as an archaic myth, a legend, etc., etc. The worldly understanding is much happier believing that Mary committed adultery than contemplating the possibility that the Holy Spirit came upon her.

I mentioned other meanings of "offense" to just to point out that words often have multiple meanings, and we need to be careful which one is used in any given context.
