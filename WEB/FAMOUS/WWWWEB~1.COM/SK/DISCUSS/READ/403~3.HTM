>In the second book of Either/Or SK discusses choosing "absolutely". He claims that the Jews did not choose God absolutely. Can someone shed some light on this notion of choosing God absolutely and how the Jews did not do that?

I'm taking a stab in the dark (or should I use the term, leap of faith? Nah...) at this, not having read Either/Or yet. But I would most likely guess he's mimicing the biblical language of many of the prophets, and that of Moses especially in Deuteronomy, that it was not the Jews who chose God, it was God who chose the Jews. One good example is Deuteronomy 7:7, "The Lord did not set his affection on you and choose you because you were more numerous than other peoples, for you were the fewest of all peoples. But it was because the Lord loved you and kept the oath he swore to your forefathers that he brought you out with a mighty hand and redeemed you from the land of slavery, from the power of Pharaoh King of Egypt."

Again, not having read Either/Or yet, this is just a guess, but it seems reasonable based on the way SK uses Scripture in so many of his other writings.
