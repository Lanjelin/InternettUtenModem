Well, I must say that Nietzsche was the true genius of the bunch. He said so himself you know, "I am a man of genius, therefore I can afford to smile or to spit at you." Oh the arrogance of the man! But, in his insanity, he had it "more together" than Sartre, Camus, Dostoevsky, KIERKEGAARD. He was the only one who got even close to the truth of it all... He was the only one who recognized the beauty of living and living in a state of nature...

"Deprived of my last veil of illusion, the power of ideas, I gaze with terror upon the void. But still, I cling to existence for the fact of mere existence is all that is left in the shattered landscape of the intellect. All reasoning is a mode of self-deception yet I cannot reason myself into a state of bliss and imagine that I can find happiness in the realm of death sunk deep in Nirvana. Oh to be alive! To vegetate stupidly, but still, to be alive and feel the warmth of the sun!"

And a poet too.
