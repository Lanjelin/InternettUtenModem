>1. It contains the dichotomous terms in their entirety - as a
union. Actuality for SK is not without a single person that positively
forges it. Thus, there is no sense to a larger actuality within
which a smaller more personal actuality might make itself known.
>
>2. Instead of "miracle," why not use SK's term of "paradox?" Faith
is the acceptance of the unacceptable. Thus, faith is defined as
the subjective response to such a given as Jesus Christ as God.
>
>You are on to an important point, I think, when you constantly recur
to wanting to say, in the "miracle," that the objective given has
its truth within it. SK's important thesis about Jesus Christ was
that His person, as it was given, was the sign of offense. His
givenness then demanded a decision: to be offended or to believe.
It is in the latter that a person takes up faith as a task.
But this would also be coincident with living, or recognizing
in the moment, the miracle: the given person Jesus presented in
a moment of time as God.
