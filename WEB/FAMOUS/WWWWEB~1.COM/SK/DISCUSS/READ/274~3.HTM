Freedom = absence of compulsion

internal/ external

Here is a loose metaphor.
A man who is free to stay out late and go to parties and chase beautiful women is obviously more free than one who does not have that option and is restrained by some means. The former can go out or stay home, while the latter is not free to go out, all he can do is stay home.

I think SK is not so much concerned with this kindof freedom -- he ismore concerned with how the first man is enslaved. True he is free to chase women, but he is a slave to women, drool runs down his chin, and everytime he sees a beautiful women, he must stay up late running after them at parties, etc etc. Now, we do not know if the second man is enslaved inthe same way because he is restrained, so he can give no external show, yet he knows on the inside.

This is an attempt to show the differnce between external and internal freedom, and SK is big at making the distinction between the internal and the external.

No matter what the external circumstances we are internally free to take the leap of faith.
