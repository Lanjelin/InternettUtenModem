Sorry, e.j., it looks as if you're still having messes.... The Internet
is by no means perfected. First of all, I don't think a generalized
evil worries me--I'm worried about the evil that each individual commits,
although I expressed it in my post (and to myself) in general terms.
I agree completely that faith cannot be directly communicated; therefore,
that's out. However, and I was going to get into this later, faith
can definitely be indirectly communicated. If one relates to God
completely, as "Anti-Climacus" argues for in Sickness Unto Death
(SUD as I'll call it in future posts), then one relates to the
world in a brighter and all together more illuminated way. However,
can such illumination indirectly lead people to faith? And naturally,
I should trust in God that all such evil will be dealt with; yet, I
wonder how one relieves temporal suffering.
(Note Bene on temporal suffering: what I mean here is
spiritual suffering within the world. As is well known, Marx defined
suffering in purely materialistic, un-individual terms, and Hegel didn't
seem to recognize suffering at all. So when I speak of evils, I so
often mean things like child abuse--a terrible evil--and not very often
things like"economic oppression of the borgiouse over the proletariat"
or whichever.)
If I'm well meaning, so be it; this all has to do with how a
Christian relates to the world, as you pointed out, e.f. And, as a
bit of a postscript while I'm thinking about it, an argument for
aesthetics (perhaps not kosher in an SK discussion group; but, I think
SK was revolting against the romantic ideal of aesthetics; besides, I
will be the first to say SK was right many, many times and the last
to say SK was right all the time): each individual must relate
to a work of art for that art to be effective. Like faith, it is something
which can be indirectly danced around, yet only the individual relates
to art in a direct way. And so, perhaps, faith can best be indirectly
communicated through art, as a direct relation must be made with art,
and therefore perhaps a more direct indirect link to faith. Am I way
off the topic? Probably. :->
