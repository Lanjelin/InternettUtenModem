The Subject: Being Alone, SK's Diary & Quell My Children
At 16:32:35 on 08/15/96, Anthony L. Pecoraro (alp@loop.com) wrote:

"A man who could bear being alone during a whole
life-time, and alone in decisions of eternal
significance , is farthest removed from the infant
and the society-person who represent the animal
definition of the human being."

--Søren Kierkegaard, The Diary of Søren Kierkegaard,
124, Being Alone, 1854

][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][][]

QUELL MY CHILDREN

Society is my child, beckoning to every refulgent call
and whim, adhering to all proclamations subjugating the
feeble to incantations of sacramental prisonment.

The multitudes are my sacrificial withdrawals,
abridging the pangs of my insufficient domain to master
the thread to my own self-proclaimed weaving.

I am the omnipotent beast -- the chimera --
speculating and planning in the face of my children's
indecision.

I reap the benefits of moral decay, masticating
every morsel of fright placating my insatiable palate.

Devouring the adjunct of my foe's veneration, I
proclaim the dictum of battling to the end, allowing no
subservient critter to masquerade askance for the
menial hope of sheading a tear in the primrose of
daylight pristine.

I am consuming all these worlds and all these
people as my delight for the eternal crucifixion that
sheds a plenitude upon my own succulent grace. And all
these lands shall be banished afar, and then so
poisoned, for never again will I allow the fresh
greenery of my imprisoned, fruitful Earth to abound for
my sickly children gone astray.

I am deranged by all my own gifts and all these
sour infants, and the seas of crying blood, and the
lands clashing to annihilation, and the trees burning
to ash with their makers hanging within, and the
forests growing to sand, and the mountains majestic no
longer... I am deranged by all of this!

Now will the welkin open up around all our toiling
gods, and then do I commence your eternal sleep; and so
all the firmament do I breathe in, holding in a whisk
all that shown; and no star will be brilliant again;
and no such fiery spirit will again give off progeny
for sickly children to dwell in deceit for me and my
ever more.

The water was my place to sway within, to gently
swirl and turn. The light was the "I" of my mind, the
weeping of my spirit. The wind was my word to say what
my babes would never hear...the sand, the play before
my feet, that I should walk softly and remember my
gentle ground...

...the world, just one small place for a moment's
sojourn.

I am treading the stairs of time only for my
brethren, moving through endless space only that you
should see.

I have given you everything, yet you have given me
less than nothing.

...the flowers were for my touch, to play with the
scent of delight...the trees, for rest; the mountains,
for vigor...

...and even more, society is my child.

--Anthony L. Pecoraro, 1-20-95 (Book 29) c.1995
