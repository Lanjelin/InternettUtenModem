At 01:34:26 on 07/17/96, Anthony L. Pecoraro (alp@loop.com) wrote:

At 19:32:01 PST on 07-12-96, Ethan Travenio wrote:

> Kierkegaard did not seek to define faith directly. His vast amount
>of work is a testament to his dedication to the medium of indirect
>communication. Let the preachers attempt to give an objective definition
>of faith but their success is illusory. Kierkegaard's brilliance
>resided in his subjective understanding, NOT an objective understanding
>of the world. One does not gain anything by faith and this is precisely
>why to have faith is the most difficult task.

This is a brilliant understanding of the
internal noumena inherent in "imageless vision":
a parallactic ambiguity for sure, yet nonetheless
an interval for pure experiencing only.
