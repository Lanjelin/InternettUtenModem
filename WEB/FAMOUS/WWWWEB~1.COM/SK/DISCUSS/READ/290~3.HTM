1. For SK, I think, existence is indeed a part of actuality, an essential part. Actuality is the fusion of the objective/existential and the subjective/conceptual. Part of the problem, for instance, of abstract thinking is that it does not contain existence, and therefore does not rise to actuality.

2. Yes, I really meant to say that Jesus Christ as God is given. That is precisely how He presented Himself. Faith is the free acceptance of this claim. The alternative is not to have this faith, to be offended at Him, in SK's terminology.
