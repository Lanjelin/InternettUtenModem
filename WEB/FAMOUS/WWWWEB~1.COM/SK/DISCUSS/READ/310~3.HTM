>Second question, re Brown's exegesis -- It seems to proceed from an assumption that 1st century Palestine had a greater concern with background biographical data than I can believe. For example, I can accept the argument that Matthew generally wanted to show Jesus as the fulfillment of Hebrew messianic prophecy, and therefore took support from Isaiah's teaching that a "virgin shall be with child." This seems in line with the cultural concerns of the time like the Essene community's focus on the Apocalyptic tradition (with an emphasis on exactly the fulfillment of messianic prophesy).
>
>But who is the community that need to hear an explanation for an early birth in another town, in the 1st century? Brown's suggestion seems to assume transmission of discrete biographical data across distance (Bethlehem to Nazareth and on to Jerusalem and beyond) and time that I don't buy. The idea that Matthew was acting as a revisionist on a discrete body of biographical data seems anachronistic -- I don't believe Matthew would care.
>
>Does Brown think the Wise Men were cobbled up to explain why Jesus was the beneficiary of foreign contributions to his cause?
