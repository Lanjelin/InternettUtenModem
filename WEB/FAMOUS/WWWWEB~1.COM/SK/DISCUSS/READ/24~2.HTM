The Subject: Temple Brazed
At 10:50:40 on 07/16/96, Anthony L. Pecoraro (alp@loop.com) wrote:

TEMPLE BRAZED

There is no loss of hunger
Only loss of virtue
Loss of body
Inevitable loss of roads never travelled

Hunger ensues
Like the hunt that gives it power
And sustains it;
Flesh vanishes
As does the certitude to transcend
The vast panorama of uncertainty

Even the limbs don't carry us
As far as we like,
Haphazardly flailing about
With little aim to consecrate
The temple brazed
Upon a lower flame of ineptitude

The gain surpasses the envy
To hold at bay
The trinkets of our
Mounting loss

I am never perplexed
At the satiety of the worldly
Yet easily and often confounded
By this enveloping matrimony
With the deficit
As if we were marching in a parade
To a stadium on high
In a procession to damn
What we know not
What we should have

Even a dainty moving of the limbs
In a dance of the body figure
Does our loss haunt us in the fore;
Truly, though,
By the guise of merriment
In which we nurture
The lull and lethargy of being

So I am weighty within
And not to your dismay
But my own conflagration
And growing consumption;
I stand fixed
Yet teetering and crumbling
By disfigured guile

We, too, seek to take solace
Rather than having it by self,
Then harboring our own
Gruesome fragmentation
-- the pain of pain --
Haunting again and anew
In a labyrinth's maze
For undignified glue
To fix it all
Yet succumbing to the
Solvency of water unpurified
Not of the riverbed
Of our birthing awakened

Even the scents of bodies
Fail to arouse
A fermenting thunder
To jar our slumber
Or placate a distorted
Charming hunger

As for the sounds of oration
In chants undeified
Or soliloquies defaming sour tongs
Or a festering dirge of delight
Celebrating a holy rigor mortis--
This can never enjoin
The beatitude of pure creation

The poetic vein is simply
A preparation
To rest within
The substratum beyond the boundaries
Or limits of exhortation
In exploration for
A wider periphery
Of human touch

The human touch is
The first from which
I care to know

--Anthony L. Pecoraro, 4-21-95 (Book 30) c.1995

The Cosmic Encyclopedia & Cryptic Poetry

Influenced by Georges Bataille, this work composes
essays, poetry, and dialectical correspondence.
It ia a 37-year work of which I have completed 16
