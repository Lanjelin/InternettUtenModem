The Subject: Re: living in the universal
At 12:16:33 on 07/10/96, D. B. Hansen (bhansen@foxnet.net) wrote:

>At the moment of faith for Abraham, in the context of the ethical,
>he is a murderer. Can a person of faith such as Abraham even exist
>in the world? Is Abraham's faith just an ideal which those of us in
>the world are supposed to model after? If so, how can we attain it
>while still living within the domain of the ethical?

A very quick reply might be yes, Abraham is the ultimate example of
devotion. However, I do not think that it is possible for the common
man to attain this final stage of existence and still maintain the
modern 'ethical' standard of society. I think this was
true nearly 2,000 years ago when the pious established monastic
dwellings. Monasteries seperated these men of complete faith from
society, most likely because it was impossible for them to commit
themselves to God as members of that common society.

You see, the whole point of absolute faith is forgo the common ethic
and surrender to God. Faith transcends ethics. Faith transcends
logic.

It is important to note that God actually stopped Abraham
from killing his son. In that light, God truly did not demand as much
as it seemed in the end -- it was just a test. The point is that
true faith requires you to, like I said, transcend ethics and logic
to serve the order of God. 

DBH
