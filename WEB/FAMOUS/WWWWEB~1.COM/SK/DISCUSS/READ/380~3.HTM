The Subject: Re: 'Offense' and the Virgin Birth
At 05:22:15 on 02/18/97, David Tye (tye@datacube.com) wrote:

At 20:28:30 PST on 02-14-97, Frank Lynch wrote:

>At 10:10:07 PST on 02-13-97, David Tye wrote:
>
>>In your earlier note, you wrote that a contemporary of Jesus had the advantage of being able to question Him. I am sincerely interested in knowing what questions you would pose to Jesus to determine whether he was God, because I can't think of any.
>
>I suppose it is important to recall that those who were next to Him not only asked the questions, but did not recognize Him when He appeared after Easter. So who could really hope to come up with a good series of questions? Any answer would have to be proven false in order to disprove that the answerer is God; the higher power that proves the first answer false then has to move on to the bonus round and be subjected to the same test. If not proven false, is God verified? I don't think so.

Good point. Another problem: If the question is to reveal that someone is God, then the answer must be one that only God can give. But if only God can give the answer, then only God must know the answer. Otherwise someone other than God could fool us into thinking he was God by giving the God-answer.

But if only God knows the answer, then I don't know the answer anymore than does anyone else. So how do I know if I have heard it if I don't know what it is?
