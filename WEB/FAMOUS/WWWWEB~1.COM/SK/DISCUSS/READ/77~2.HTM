The Subject: The Influence of Kierkegaard
At 23:18:09 on 08/31/96, Chris White (cwhite@arts.adelaide.edu.auChr) wrote:

Given the recent renaissance of Hannah Arendt's thought and the amount
of material being published on her work, the inevitable question of
influences comes to the fore. Unsurprisingly, I suppose, I found
the shadow of Kierkegaard in her work.
I am interested to know if anyboby has ever pursued the link between
Hannah Arendt's understanding of the world and that of Kierkegaard.
I became interested in this subject matter when I discovered their
common respect for Lessing. (See Hannah Arendt, Men in Dark Times, Chap 1).
Arendt always maintained that Kierkegaard was her first influence but
references to him in her Life of the Mind come in at around about one.
Flicking through articles on Arendt and Kierkegaard no-one seems
interested in outlining their view of this interaction. They all seem -
in terms of Arendtian scholarship, that is - interested in Heidegger's
influence and 'love'.
Anyway, has anyone got any suggestions, or something they have found
along the way?
I am interested in any response of this matter and I hope that this is
a suitable topic for this newly discovered and much appreciated forum.

Chris White
