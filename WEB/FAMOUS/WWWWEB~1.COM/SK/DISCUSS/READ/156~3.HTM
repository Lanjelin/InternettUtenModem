Christian Existentialism - an interesting question although Iím still not too sure about identifying Zen with existentialism or reducing Camusí Stranger to someone acting just cos of the heat. My impression that there was something else going on.
Briefly - my own understanding of Kierkegaardís Christian existentialism is that he sought to dis-associate religious faith from reason. One does not ëbelieveí because of any logical imperatives or scientific methods or systematic philosophies or theologies/ rather, in order to believe, one leaps into the absurd inspite of (not because of) what reason may dictate.
It is the realisation of the fundamental absurdity of existence which links Kierkegaardís thinking to Camus and Satre. Camusí book the Myth of Sisyphus explores this further.
Iíd like to stress that the above is not written in great confidence - Iíve been reading Kierkegaard for the last ten years and also the writings of other existentialists. Long enough to realise that the association of religion to existential philosophy raises some very complex and fundamental questions that go right to the core of that ëphilosophyí. I wonder what would have happened if Kierkegaard and Nietzsche had to stand in the same room together??
