At 02:21:32 on 10/05/96, Anthony L. Pecoraro (alp@loop.com) wrote:

At 13:11:57 PST on 10-03-96, Tillett wrote:

>But when I have only a few lines to impart meaning, I must turn to some short-hand tools which help me to finish the thought: thus I use...

The transcript of meaning, for extended understanding and
communication of knowledge, is of primary importance.
Perhaps not in a letter to a friend or to an angst-ridden
poet whose matrimonial bond is with grief itself, but for
a legacy of comprehension to be imparted, for toys for future
hominid to play with, completing the scroll is but our gift.
We can let others bequeath the burden being succinct.

You are wonderfully open and curious: This is a flavor not
often at my dinner table; lately my fodder has been lacking
in nutrients, but your condiments are most welcome. The laymen
is the philosopher's aperitive.

Again, I won't dispute SK's beliefs. Eastern wisdom, as well
as the Greek wisdom as you noted, speaks of the a universe of
duality, of foreground and background, of the Absolute and
manifestation: Creation is fully apprehended through knowledge,
arising from direct experience, of the Absolute--the changing
and the unchanged you so aptly noted.

What is constant in terms of perception is dependant on what
school of thought one adheres to, and then, schools of knowingness
antecedent to thought (the Eastern); but yes, that constant is there
-- must be there -- or life would indeed crumble, as do stars,
higher globes, and universes in due course. Perception of this
"constant," however, is a matter of evolution -- mutation -- of
our species. That it may be named "ethical-religious" is, in my
mind, time-bound, meaning such analytical comprehension will alter
as being proliferates through out the starry panorama. But for now,
it is quite useful for brute to toy with.


"The greater purposiveness of the wondrous universal display that
is meagerly apparent to viscous eyes defies explanation on an
intellectual level. What we see out there is the outcome -- never
experiencing its cause. The cause of all things is the cause of
every being's delight to manifestation. Such cause being the same,
it may be experienced by all beings according to their
constitution, one of which is human-creature, and others more
human than creature-like, and further, the human-natural. Each
succeeding mutation transcends the influence of any possible
meaning which was confined to its particular state of existence."

--ALP, 11/23/90 (Book 20, {Trouble}, p.4)


Actualities emerge as potentialities manifest: a congealing eventuality from a state of complete repose. From the base
of this constant you speak of, reality becomes a durable presence
for mind to be centered in, transcending, then, from this point
(center) in presence here and now, our apparent reality.

What kind of work do you do?

ALP, 10-5-96 1:x (Book 38)
