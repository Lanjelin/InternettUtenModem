Well, for me (as if you all wanted to know), because I agree with Christianity, because I see the importance of the individual to God and the life of active love, of a God who created the universe and every soul in it, as the ideals I think exists. I have fundamental disagreements with how Buddha and Mohammed approached faith; obviously, they too dealt with subjective knowledge, so the basis of calling them wrong is very shaky, and I wouldn't do that--all I can say is that I am personally a Christian because it goes hand in hand with my observations about the world around me.

Of course, any of you can (and should) argue that this is truly silly; that I'm a Christian simply because I feel like being a Christian, etc., etc. I'm going to try to defend my position in a discussion of Don Quijote up above; but I'm a Christian for purely subjective reasons. At least it's the subjectivity I believe in.
