At 16:58:30 PST on 09-02-96, Valerie Schjenken wrote:

>First of all, I am a woman (well, really a girl) with a question. I know this is for
>philosophical discussion and all, but what does everyone feel about the role of SK's
>father in the development of his melancholy? I mean, obviously, there's a correlation,
>but do you think it was genetic or psychological? And what are you conclusions about
>what the great sin in his father's life was? Was it his sexual promiscuity or cursing
>God as a poor shepherd boy? I'm still in his biography, but as soon as I get to his

>
I don't know, Valerie. I've heard both theories that it was the sexual prmiscuity
or cursing God; I think the secret is probably immaterial, though, much like the
"MacGuffin" in an Alfred Hitchcock film--it helps drive the plot, but knowing what it
is isn't essential. SK's dad probably wouldn't have found something to obsessively
base his melancholy on (and pass it to his children) anyway you look at it. Johannes
does have a point, though; maybe it was something biological; it depends on whether
you buy a lot of modern medical psychology or not. I mean, today, they might well have
diagnosed SK with Seasonal Affective Disorder and told him to go to Italy with more
sunlight. But, Valerie, tell me: is SK's melancholy at the heart of his work? I think
one can adopt a lot of what SK says and still be radiantly joyful. (Then again, I live
in Arizona, where there's a great deal of sunlight). What's your take on this Valerie?
