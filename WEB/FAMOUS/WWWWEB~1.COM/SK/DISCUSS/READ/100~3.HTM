ANIMA'S BLAZING AURORA

I stare blank
Gleaming with displeasure

A thicker air
Mimics inaudible battles
Upon unventured shores

We fence with one another
And with words
Not allowed to dream

Vanquished are the vocables deciphered
Transcribed by soul's
Cryptic eye

It becomes more pleasurable
The stare
When words burn my skin
By anima's blazing aurora

--Anthony L. Pecoraro, 9-5-96 1:2 (Book 37) c.1996


DEAR CROSSING

The torn cloth
Of burning aurora
Fades before
The likeness of destiny

Destiny is an aged dear
In mist-free meadows
Wending its way
From torn cloths
Scattered astray

My shepherded meadow
Cradles the oldest of deer

--Anthony L. Pecoraro, 9-11-96 1:3 (Book 37) c.1996
