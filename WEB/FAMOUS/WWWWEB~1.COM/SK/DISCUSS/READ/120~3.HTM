At 09:37:13 on 09/30/96, capt. (isolder@worldnet.att.net) wrote:

God had promised Abraham the fatherhood of a nation and a land to call his own. Isaac was going to be the lineage through which God establish His people. Abraham knew this before he began his journey in faith to his new home. I can only imagine what Isaac must've been to his father: Abraham's future and reason for a new life away from the land of his ancestor's. And now, God is asking him to destroy all this, all this that Isaac stood for, not to mention whatever feelings there were between them. Keeping in mind that Isaac was a promise Abraham laughed at when he was told at what age Sarah would concieve. This far gone succesion of events was going to be stript from him for apparently no reason. But with faith like a child Abraham went through with the destruction, to be left empty hearted in a foreign land with nothing tangible to justify him being there. Social and spiritual suicidal all in one, to the delight of God.
