The Subject: Abraham
At 20:29:37 on 08/05/96, J. Climacus (brettdel@mail.aros.net) wrote:

I think viewing Abraham as a model of correct behavior is to attempt to recast his actions in an ethical framework. I think the point of Abraham's dilemma is that the demands of faith are incommensurate with a life in, as you say, the polis. As a professor of mine once said, there is little to extract from the Abraham/Isaac story except maybe: It is a terrible to be in the presence of the Living God. For me, the story of Abraham, to the extent that it can be made into any kind of sense, is that the demands of God and faith will always be outside the matrix of conceptual thought. Ethical categories simply don't apply. 

J. Climacus
