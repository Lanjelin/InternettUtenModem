At 14:09:33 PST on 11-16-96, Rob wrote:

>
I am having real problems trying to understand the part of "Truth Is Subjectivity" where it goes into truth and into the quote "Faith is an objective uncertainty held fast in an appropriation-process of the most passionate inwardness". I am totally lost!!! Can someone explain this to me???

This quote is one of the cornerstones of SK's thought, and can't be easily explained, nor easily understood. I do not claim to understand entirely. But I think it's perhaps helpful to distinguish between "facts" and "truth." For Kierkegaard, facts are merely loutish, largely uninteresting, objectively existing items for our awareness that do not excite much interest and certainly little passion. They do not create any possibilities for disagreement because they are simply indisputable (assuming, of course, one is not arguing with Descartes). Truth, for SK, are things that we cannot know, and can never be (or at least should not be) reduced to fact. These are the things we can get excited about because they cannot be proven. They are the ideas of God, morality, justice, peace, meaning. Although we may enlist facts in support of various theories in support our views on these ideas, we can never prove them in the same way we can prove 2 + 2 = 4. These are ideas to die for. These are ideas that must be held fast in an appropriation process (they must be constantly appropriated because they cannot be proven) of the most passionate inwardness (inwardness because outwardness is the path to objectivity, to the world of brute facts).
Or so it seems to me. Of course, we welcome contrasting views from responsible individuals.
