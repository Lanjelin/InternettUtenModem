>No--it is impossible to "bastardize" it, but it is quite easy to overlook it. Another theme that K. develops but is often overlooked or dismissed is the idea of an eternal, ineffable God "operating" in a temporal zone of death and despair is "logically absurd." K. makes it quite clear that God did not create man's despair; by implication, the tautology results: God did not create the world (where man's existential despair is played out). This is stunning and dismaying at the same time. One can only accept what one cannot understand. The route back to certainty is through the despair: resting "transparently" while undergoing what seems to be its opposite. It is important to realize that what the mind makes true in the mind denies its opposite. The world of defiance is an ancient world--the defiance is total, not partial. The denial is layered by how the world has been structured to keep the denial (i.e., the guilt) in place, with breaches severely punished. Theology is nothing more than man telling God how to think. Education is a conditioning of the mind to serve the world's idea of itself. Each "role" a person plays within the world serves to keep the despair hidden, secret, with occasional breakthroughs, but nothing that seriously undermines the thought that maintains it.
