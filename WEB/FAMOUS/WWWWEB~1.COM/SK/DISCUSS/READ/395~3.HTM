At 10:28:04 PST on 02-06-97, J. Climacus wrote:

>
>
> When I think about why certain experiences, certain aphorisms or certain people have become important to me, I see myself stumbling through a murky darkness -- shapes of shadow and light whizzing past me, all unrecognizable at first. But as I move through the fog, I note that some of the passing lights are somehow more congenial, more friendly, and I find that with a little effort I can keep pace with them (or is it that they wait for me to catch up?). And I find that if I stay close to these sprightly esssences, they illuminate, albeit modestly, the landscape around me. Over time, I collect more of these incandescent companions (or are they simply letting me tag along?), and I hold them fast ("in an appropriation process of the most passionate inwardness"). I realize that with each new light, I make slight incursions into the unfathomable sea of infinite black, expanding the boundaries of my tiny cocoon of coherence. Over time, I begin to feel at home in what is an otherwise hostile and forbidding cosmos. Certainly, Kierkegaard is a lantern that grows steadily brighter, in tiny increments, the more I read.
>


Yes - I've been thinking how does one 'talk' about Kierkegaard - when his thinking is so mercurial & so profound - but obviously it is senseless to try and turn him into a 'guru' or something cos his whole emphasis is the primacy of the subjective experience - ie. yours & mine. Not his.

At the moment I am plowing through 'Stages in Life's Way' - plowing in that it takes so long - so often K will say something that stops me absolutely short & I have to put the book down & let that thought seep into me. Such a contrast to the values of the contemporary millieu - where money is all that counts apparently. K is signifying something 'else' - much deeper. Disturbing and exhilerating.

Enjoy.
