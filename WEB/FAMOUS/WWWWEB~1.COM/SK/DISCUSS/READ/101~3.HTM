The Subject: Re: Spheres
At 04:36:09 on 09/19/96, Tillett (tillett@usa.net) wrote:

At 08:10:22 PST on 09-14-96, J. Climacus wrote:

>There is some confusion ...

Concentric circles is the picture K uses. As one leaps to a higher sphere, the lower sphere is preserved unchanged, except that now it is utilized in accordance with the peculiar telos of the higher sphere. My short-hand way of recollecting this notion while reading K is that each stage is marked by what the person regards as absolute: in the esthetic, personal pleasure is the absolute; in the ethical, self-responsibility in choosing (resigning oneself to) the universal; and in the religious, self-responsibility in choosing (and resigning oneself to) God.

A couple of randomly selected points: (1) Within this concentric circle theory, in what sense is the lower sphere preserved unchanged, after one has leaped to a higher stage? Either/Or II is the first (but not the only) formulation of K's position. And (2) re: your question relating to the pychological concept of a "mid-life crisis:" I think the whole point of the ethical-religious spheres is to obtain a fixed point which does not change as life proceeds. One's mood and outlook changes esthetically, not ethically and religiously. By definition, moods are not "understandable," they are the random fruits of life lived within the temporal locus of the psycho-biological aspect of the personality.
