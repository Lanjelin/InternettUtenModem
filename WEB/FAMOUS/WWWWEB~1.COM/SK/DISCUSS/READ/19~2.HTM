The Subject: Re: benefits and losses of faith
At 09:29:06 on 07/08/96, James Smith (sjames@tron.cochise.cc.az.us) wrote:

At 11:17:23 PST on 07-06-96, EF wrote:

>Possible losses: life, soul, selfdetermination, development, overview
>etc.
>Possible benefits: ?
Why, the absolute, naturally. In fact, I'd like to say that only through
faith in God, can we develop, can we find our personal fire of self
determination, can we find the miraclous spirit God has created of us;
in short, through faith in God alone can one truly find life. Now,
perhaps in faith, one may resign such things for a moment; such is
described by SK's Knight of Infinite Resignation (O.K. Johannes de
Silento's Knight), and St. John of the Cross's "Dark Night of the Soul."
However, only through faith can one possibly achieve life; not just
life everlasting, but life beautiful and wonderous. Now, if "faith"
has led to death, soullessness, blind obeidience, lack of development,
etc., etc., it's hardly faith at all. Perhaps we need to define faith;
but, ha, ha, that would impossible. Well, I hope that helps answer the
question; disagreements (as well as agreements) are quite welcome, though. :->
