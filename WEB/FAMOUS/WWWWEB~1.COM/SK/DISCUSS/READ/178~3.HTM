turning back to rectify the situation, he keeps going, going and going;
indeed modern man has gotten very far inthis manner -- he is very
"sucessful". Modern MAn has become objective, meaning he does not
realize the importance of APPROPRIATION. And something who does not
know appropriation cannot feed itself, therefore it goes on and on,
eating away, further and further...GET IT? No consumation, no rest.

>DO YOU REALLY THINK THAT CHRISTIANS NEED MORE TRADITION? I SAY LESS TRADITION AND MORE SELF

I am surprised that you were able to read this out of what I said.
I don't know if more tradition is needed, but I do think tradition
has a valuable place. Kierkegaard did not discredit Religiousness A
with Religiousness B. Some think that in today's rationalized world,
there is no room for religious belief, especially if it deals in
metaphysical of mythical language. I, however, think that the jury
is still out. I'm not so sure that we can replace the meanings
involved in symbols with rational concepts. The goal, however, is to
constantly re-interpret those meanings. This is the task of Church
Dogmatics and Systematic Theology. Kierkegaard might disagree on this
point. The job is never done, however, and here is wherer existential
subjectivity can be employed to guard
against hegemony, fanaticism, etc.
