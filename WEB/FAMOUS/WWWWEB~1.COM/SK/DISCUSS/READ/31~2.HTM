The Subject: Logic / reason and faith
At 09:18:21 on 07/24/96, mike (HTMSS@AOL.COM) wrote:

I see no major conflict in using reason to defend my beleifs in sk's
writings. Most often, defenders of reason dismiss faith by setting up
a straw-man to stand for God, defining Him as mere consciousness
and knocking him down with their insistence on primacy of existence
over conciousness, this is true but doesn't apply to God. Morality
can be derived from objective analysis of existence for God created
the existence we study. The suspension of ethics for teleological
faith as defined in Fear and Trembling is a case where faith would
take presidence over reason, but I've found that a conflict between
reasonable ethics and Christian doctrine often does not arise, and
ONLY when it does is truth subjective. One must remember that truth
on earth is objective- but one is judged alone, without the benefit
of any temporal relationships, thus truth would then be subjective.
This distinction is essential when one would concern oneself with
politics or any other field where morality is the concern of
a group of people rather than an individual. Objective guidlines
are a must.
