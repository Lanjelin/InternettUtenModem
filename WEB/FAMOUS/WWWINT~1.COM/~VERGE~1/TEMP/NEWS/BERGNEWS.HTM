Bergman interview on Swedish TV

An interview, entitled Private conversation with Ingmar Bergman, was telecast on Swedish TV on 8 February. It was half an hour long and very interesting. Bergman was very forthcoming, so the interviewer (Gunnar Bergdahl, head of the Göteborg Film Festival) didn't have to ask very many questions. However the interview was not very personal, with the main subject being Swedish films of today. For more details, check out the Expressen article about the session.


Bergman Retrospective in Göteborg

The Göteborg Cinematheque is showing many of Bergman's early films this winter and spring. A screening schedule is available.


Bergman directs Larmar och gör sig till

Bergman has directed a Swedish television production of his play Larmar och gör sig till, from his book Femte akten ["The Fifth Act"]. Börje Ahlstedt stars, with Bergman himself in a walk-on appearance. Shooting was completed around the end of November 1996, and the project is now in post-production.


Enskilda Samtal: the novel

Bergman's sequel to The Best Intentions was published in November 1996, and is currently available in bookstores. The English-language version is entitled Private Confessions. One chapter ("Confession") of the novel was excerpted and published in the 11 November 1996 issue of The New Yorker.


Enskilda Samtal: the movie

Liv Ullmann's production of Bergman's novel was broadcast on Swedish television in December 1996. Check out Simon's review of this production! Outside the Nordic area, one segment (entitled Confessions) will be exhibited in a feature-film version in 1997.


Bergman docu-drama in the works

Danish director Lars von Trier will film a docu-drama on Bergman's life, starring Ernst-Hugo Järegård. Production is to begin later this year on this project for Danish television. It is expected to be broadcast in 1998.
