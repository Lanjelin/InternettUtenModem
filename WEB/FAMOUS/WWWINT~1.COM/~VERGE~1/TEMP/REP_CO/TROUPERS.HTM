Allan Edwall

b. 1924 in Rödön, Sweden. d. 1997.
An accomplished character actor, and among Bergman's longest-serving collaborators. "One of the most versatile actors in Sweden" (Peter Cowie), Edwall also turned his hand to directing in later years.


Anders Ek

b. 1916 in Göteborg, Sweden. d. 1978.
A vivid supporting screen actor, perhaps most memorable as Frost the clown in The Naked Night. "He was a tough and critical colleague; at once gentle and ruthless" (Ingmar Bergman).


Gertrud Fridh

b. 1921 in Göteborg, Sweden.
"A classical actress of considerable power (Hedda Gabler in Bergman's stage production, for instance), Miss Fridh has an uncanny flair for suggesting a submerged eroticism and vulnerabilty beneath her usually aloof and sarcastic poise."—Peter Cowie


Jarl Kulle

b. 1927 in Ekeby, Sweden.
A popular star in Sweden. Most notable in comedy (Smiles of a Summer Night, The Devil's Eye), but also superb in drama (such as the title role in Bergman's 1984 production of King Lear at the Royal Dramatic Theatre).




Source: Film in Sweden by Peter Cowie; Images: My Life in Film by Ingmar Bergman.
