January 30, 1997

Anthony Hopkins signs up for 'Amistad'

From the Hollywood Reporter:

Anthony Hopkins will play his second former American president in a movie with his casting as John Quincy Adams in DreamWorks SKG's "Amistad." The studio confirmed the casting, marking the first actor deal to close on the film that DreamWorks principal Steven Spielberg will direct beginning next month. While unconfirmed by DreamWorks, it is believed the studio is in negotiations with Morgan Freeman, Matthew McConaughey and Djimon Hounsou to play major roles in the slave-ship drama. Hopkins, most recently Oscar-nominated for his performance in the title role of Oliver Stone's "Nixon," was last seen in "Surviving Picasso." His most recent projects are 20th Century Fox's "Bookworm" and TriStar's "The Mask of Zorro," which Spielberg is exec producing. "Amistad" is a true story of the 1839 revolt aboard the Spanish slave ship Amistad. The Africans were captured off the coast of New England and landed in a trial before the Supreme Court in which Adams represented the Africans. Rick Nicita of CAA represents the actor. (Kirk Honeycutt)
