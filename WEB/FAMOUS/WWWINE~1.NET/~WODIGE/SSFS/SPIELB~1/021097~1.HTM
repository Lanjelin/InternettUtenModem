February 10, 1997

'Lost World' campaign seeks to end the T-hex

From The Hollywood Reporter:

NEW YORK -- Universal Pictures pulled out all the stops at the 1997 American International Toy Fair, with appearances Monday by Jeff Goldblum and other cast members, as it announced a licensing blitz for its biggest property of the year, "The Lost World: Jurassic Park." The studio said it signed up Burger King, Mercedes-Benz, Kodak and General Mills as part of a multimillion-dollar marketing campaign for the dinosaurs of "The Lost World." The campaign could help revive what has been a tough property to market. Dinosaurs are difficult to turn into a brand for kids because one dinosaur looks much like another. As a result, Universal received less than $100 million from the licensing of "Jurassic Park," according to a source familiar with the licensing campaign. (Andrew Collier)
