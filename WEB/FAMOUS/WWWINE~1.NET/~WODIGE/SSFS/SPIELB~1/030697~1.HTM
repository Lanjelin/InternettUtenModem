March 6, 1997

Full info on 'Amistad'

From ClariNet Electronic News Service:

UNIVERSAL CITY, Calif.--(ENTERTAINMENT WIRE)--Feb. 18, 1997--

Principal photography commenced Tuesday on the historical drama "Amistad," director Steven Spielberg's first feature under the DreamWorks SKG banner, it was announced Tuesday by Walter Parkes and Laurie MacDonald, co-heads of DreamWorks Pictures.

The film will be produced by DreamWorks Pictures in association with HBO Pictures. Debbie Allen ("Fame," "A Different World") and Colin Wilson ("The Lost World," "Casper") will produce, with Parkes and MacDonald serving as executive producers. The screenplay is by David Franzoni ("Citizen Cohn") and Academy Award-winner Steve Zaillian ("Schindler's List," "Searching for Bobby Fischer").

The film features an international cast led by (in alphabetical order) Morgan Freeman, Nigel Hawthorne, Djimon Honsou, Anthony Hopkins, Matthew McConaughey and Pete Postlethwaite.

Set in 1839, "Amistad" is the true story of a revolt on the Spanish slave ship Amistad, whose 'cargo' of 53 Africans rebelled against their captors. Captured off the coast of Connecticut when their desperate attempt to sail home failed, the Africans are forced to stand trial for the murders of the crew that had held them prisoner.

To win their freedom, the Africans -- with the help of local abolitionists -- find themselves taking on not just the Spanish crown but the entire American system of justice. They are joined in their fight by former U.S. President John Quincy Adams, who is willing to take on their cause and fight their battle all the way to the U.S. Supreme Court.

Allen first brought the property, which she has had for more than a decade, to DreamWorks' attention. In the ensuing development over the last three years, Allen has spearheaded the creation of an "Amistad" research department which continues to amass a prodigious amount of historical documentation, consulting with some of the leading scholars and cultural historians of 19th Century America and the Mende tribe of West Africa.

"We are proud to launch Steven Spielberg's first DreamWorks project with 'Amistad.' It's a great story of courage and will, to be told by one of our greatest storytellers," said Parkes in making the announcement. "It is only fitting that we've begun the filming of this important episode in American history during Black History Month," he added.

Freeman stars as the abolitionist Joadson. After years of stage and television work, Freeman came to moviegoers' attention in the 1980 prison drama "Brubaker." He received the first of three Oscar nominations for his work in the 1987 film "Street Smart." A second Oscar nomination came when Freeman reprised his Obie-winning stage role as a Southern chauffeur in "Driving Miss Daisy" (1989).

His role in the 1995 film "The Shawshank Redemption" earned him a third Oscar nomination. Among his other roles are, "Glory," "Unforgiven," "Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves," and the upcoming "Kiss the Girls" and "The Flood." In 1993, Freeman made his feature directorial debut with "Bopha!".

Hawthorne stars as the pro-slavery U.S. President Martin Van Buren. Hawthorne earned an Academy Award nomination for his performance in "The Madness of King George" (1994). Hawthorne had originated the role on stage with Britain's Royal National Theatre, winning Best Actor honors from the Olivier Awards, the Evening Standard and Timeout Magazine.

On the American stage, he received the 1991 Tony Award for Best Actor for the Broadway production of "Shadowlands." He has won four BAFTA awards for Best Actor in a comedy, two for "Yes Minister" and two for "Yes Prime Minister." In addition to his numerous credits on British stage and television, Hawthorne's feature films roles include "Demolition Man," "Foxfire," "Gandhi," and most recently, "Murder in Mind."

Honsou portrays Cinque, the leader of the Africans' rebellion against their captors. A native of Benin, West Africa, Honsou was living in Paris when he was discovered by fashion designer Thierry Mughler. A successful modeling career followed, and Honsou soon began appearing in music videos such as Madonna's "Express yourself," directed by David Fincher, and Janet Jackson's "Love Will Never Do Without You," directed by Herb Ritts. He made his film debut in "Stargate," and has recently completed work on three upcoming features, "Ill Gotten Gains," "Ink" and "Deep Rising."

Hopkins portrays former U.S. President John Quincy Adams. Hopkins won the 1992 Oscar for Best Actor for his role as Dr. Hannibal Lecter in the film "The Silence of the Lambs." In 1993, he received an Oscar nomination for "Remains of the Day" and another nomination in 1995 for "Nixon." He has received two Emmy Awards, the first for his role as Bruno Hauptmann in the 1976 television movie, "The Lindbergh Kidnapping Case," and later for his role as Adolf Hitler in "The Bunker" (1981). He was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II in 1993.

McConaughey portrays Baldwin, the first attorney willing to take on the Africans' cause. McConaughey exploded on the scene in the hit 1996 movie, "A Time to Kill." His previous film credits include "Dazed and Confused," "Lone Star," "Boys On The Side" and "Larger Than Life." He recently completed a starring role opposite Jodie Foster in Robert Zemeckis' sci-fi thriller "Contact."

Postlethwaite plays Saunders, the top prosecutor representing the government's case against the Africans. Postlethwaite most recently collaborated with Spielberg on "The Lost World."

He previously garnered great acclaim for his role as an unjustly imprisoned man in "In the Name of the Father" and has also appeared in "James and the Giant Peach," "Usual Suspects," "Dragonheart," "Last of the Mohicans," "Aliens III," "Waterland" and "Hamlet."

Postlethwaite's numerous theater credits include Royal Shakespeare Co. productions of "A Midsummer Night's Dream," "Macbeth," "King Lear," "Henry V" and "Richard III."

Spielberg has directed and/or produced six of the 20 top-grossing films of all time. He recently completed filming "The Lost World: Jurassic Park" for Universal Pictures. "Jurassic Park" is currently the highest-grossing film worldwide.

In 1994, Spielberg won the Academy Award for Best Picture and Best Director for "Schindler's List," which also earned him his second Directors Guild award. His first DGA Award was for "The Color Purple."

Following "Amistad," Spielberg will begin production of the World War II drama "Saving Private Ryan," a co-venture between DreamWorks SKG and Paramount Studios starring Tom Hanks.

The behind-the-scenes team includes director of photography Janusz Kaminski ("The Lost World," "Schindler's List"), production designer Rick Carter ("The Lost World," "Forrest Gump," "Jurassic Park), editor Michael Kahn ("The Lost World," "Schindler's List," "Jurassic Park") and costumer designer Ruth Carter ("Rosewood," "Cobb," "Malcolm X").

"Amistad" will be filmed on location in Los Angeles, New England and the Caribbean. The film is currently scheduled for a late-1997 release.
