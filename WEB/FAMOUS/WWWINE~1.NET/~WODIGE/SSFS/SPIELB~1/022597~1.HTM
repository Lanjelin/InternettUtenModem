February 25, 1997

65 million witness NBC 'Schindler'

From The Hollywood Reporter:

A 3 1/2-hour black-and-white film about the Holocaust attracted 65 million people to NBC Sunday night. The network estimates that's how many viewers tuned in to at least six minutes of its 7:30-11 p.m. presentation of Steven Spielberg's Oscar-winning "Schindler's List." The entire feature broadcast averaged about 40 million viewers, nearly doubling in one night the total number of people who saw the movie in U.S. theaters (25 million). "Schindler's List" attracted a 23.8 rating/34 share in the 36 metered markets, according to Nielsen Media Research. This is the highest overnight rating for a movie on any network since the NBC telecast of Spielberg's "Jurassic Park" on May 7, 1995, which garnered an overnight 25.3/37. The film brought additional viewers to broadcast TV and did not hurt the other networks' performances appreciably. Among them, the four broadcast networks captured 78% of the television audience. ABC's Sunday movie, "Volcano: Fire on the Mountain," scored a 12.0/17 in the metered markets, while CBS' "Night Sins" movie posted a 9.9/14. (Lisa de Moraes)
