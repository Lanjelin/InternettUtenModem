March 6, 1997

On location info for 'Amistad'

From The Newport Daily News, (sent in by Darrin Robinson):

AMISTAD: ON LOCATION IN NEWPORT, RHODE ISLAND (Newport Daily News)

Steven Speilberg and the actors in Amistad, are expected to arrive from principal filming in Los Angeles on Tuesday, March 4th. Filming is expected to begin on Wednesday, March 5th at the Old Colony House and in and around Washington Square in Newport, Rhode Island.

Dreamworks SKG location manager Nick Bernstein and crews have spent the past weeks changing parts of Downtown Newport into a working set.. crews began placing dirt on the sidewalks and have already completed the removal of electrical fixtures and store signs. Monday will be a busy day for crews as they put the finishing touches on the set.

From The Providence Journal, The Newport Daily News, and 'JCT' (sent in by JCT):

From all accounts, shooting on Amistad should begin Wednesday of next week. Hundreds of local residents, who have been cast as extras, are gathering on a daily basis in the Rotunda at Easton' Beach for costume fittings. Of the thousands of costume pieces being used in the film most came from England and Italy though I did hear a rumor that Spielberg himself purchased a few vintage hats and other pieces from local antique merchants.

Matthew McConaughey and Morgan Freeman are expected to come to Newport to shoot scenes but Anthony Hopkins and Nigel Hawthorne are not scheduled to be on location at the present time.

Much of the historic district downtown is being renovated to look like the 1840's which isn't too difficult because Newport has a wealth of well preserved Early American architecture. There has been a considerable stir about the fake jail being built at Queen Anne Square. Some (a stodgy few) locals are in an uproar because "nothing should be built on city property" but most residents have welcome the changes, especially the dismantling of money eating parking meters and ugly signage. Several parents of the free-spirited teenagers who typically fill the lawn below Trinity Church are secretly hoping the jail remains after filming is completed. (I count myself among those parents!)
