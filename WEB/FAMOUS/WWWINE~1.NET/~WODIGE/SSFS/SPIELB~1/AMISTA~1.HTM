PLOT:

Period drama based on the true story of a mutiny on the slave ship Amistad in 1839, centering on Cinque, an African captive who led the rebellion. When the captives' attempt to sail home failed, they were captured off the Atlantic coast.

The story follows their struggle in the U.S. as they fight their case in the court. Former president John Quincy Adams defends them and takes their battle to the Supreme Court.


DEVELOPMENTS:

March 6, 1997--Full information - character descriptions, extended crew info, cast backgrounds.

March 6, 1997--On location in Rhode Island, several reports here...

February 21, 1997--"Ill Gotten Gains," a similar film that premiered in Africa, shares a similar plot, the same ship as a set, and Djimon Hounsou as a star.

February 19, 1997--Cast is finalized. Hawthorne, Postlethwaite are announced; McConaughey, Freeman, Hounsou confirmed. Filming begins within the week.

January 30, 1997--Anthony Hopkins signs to play John Quincy Adams.

January 25, 1997--The New England location that Steven Spielberg will be using for Amistad will be Newport, Rhode Island. Filming will commence there during the month of March. (announced on a RI local TV station, report sent in by Keith Brava.)

January 19, 1997--LA Times reports in Calendar section that the film should be released sometime in Fall 1997.

January 7, 1997---Matthew McConaughey may star.

November 7, 1996---Production is expected to begin in February, with filming taking place in Los Angeles, New England and the Caribbean. After ``Amistad,'' he will direct Tom Hanks in ``Saving Private Ryan.''
