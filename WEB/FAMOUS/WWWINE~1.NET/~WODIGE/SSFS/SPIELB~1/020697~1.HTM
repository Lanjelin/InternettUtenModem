February 6, 1997

Production began January 27 on 'Zorro'

From ClariNet Electronic News Service:

CULVER CITY, Calif.--(ENTERTAINMENT WIRE)--Jan. 22, 1997-- Production begins on TriStar Pictures' "The Mask of Zorro," starring Antonio Banderas and Academy Award-winner Anthony Hopkins on Monday, Jan. 27 in Mexico City, it was announced Wednesday by TriStar president Robert Cooper.

A TriStar Pictures/Amblin Entertainment production, "The Mask of Zorro" is directed by Martin Campbell, who garnered critical raves for the hit James Bond thriller "GoldenEye," which grossed more than $350 million worldwide. The film also stars Catherine Zeta Jones ("The Phantom") and Stuart Wilson ("The Age of Innocence"). Doug Claybourne and David Foster are the producers. Terry Rossio & Ted Elliott, John Eskow and David Ward wrote the screenplay for Steven Spielberg's Amblin Entertainment film division, headed by Walter Parkes and Laurie MacDonald.

"The Mask of Zorro" is a sweeping adventure of love and honor, of tragedy and triumph set against Mexico's fight for independence from the iron fist of Spain. It's 20 years since Don Diego de la Vega (Hopkins) successfully fought against Spanish oppression as the legendary romantic hero Zorro. Now he must find a successor to stop Raphael Montero (Wilson), the powerful former Spanish governor who has returned to reclaim the country for himself. Banderas stars as Alejandro Murietta, a drifter with a troubled past whom Diego transforms into Zorro, the fearless romantic hero he once was. Armed with his mask, sword, whip and jet-black stallion Tornado, the new Zorro embarks on a mission to stop Montero's plot to reclaim California.

"'The Mask of Zorro' has been a tremendously important project to this studio and we are excited to move forward with such a group of distinguished actors and filmmakers involved, particularly Martin Campbell, who virtually reinvented the James Bond movie with 'GoldenEye,'" said Cooper.

The renowned behind-the-scenes team includes production designer Cecilia Montiel ("Desperado"), costume designer Graciela Mazon ("Desperado") and director of photography Phil Meheux ("GoldenEye," "The Saint"). Glenn Randall Jr. ("Species," "Raiders of the Lost Ark") will coordinate the film's substantial stuntwork, with Robert Anderson serving as sword master.

In addition to starring in the current award-winning "Evita," Banderas has starred in such films as "Philadelphia," "Two Much," "Never Talk to Strangers" and "Desperado". The star of five films with Spanish director Pedro Almodovar, including "Tie Me Up! Tie Me Down!" and "Women on the Verge of a Nervous Breakdown," and more than 20 other features, Banderas is now recognized internationally as one of the most exciting actors of his generation.

Hopkins most recently starred in two highly acclaimed films based on historical characters: the legendary painter Pablo Picasso in "Surviving Picasso," and the controversial U.S. President Richard Nixon in "Nixon," a role which garnered him an Academy Award nomination. His filmography includes some of the most critically hailed films of recent times, including "Legends of the Fall," "Shadowlands," "The Remains of the Day," "Bram Stoker's Dracula," "Howards End" and an Academy Award-winning performance in "The Silence of the Lambs". He recently completed starring in the action/thriller "Bookworm" for release later this year.

Jones stars as Diego de la Vega's beautiful daughter Elena. The Welsh-born actress recently starred in the adventure thriller "The Phantom". One of the United Kingdom's most popular actresses, Zeta Jones came to prominence in the British TV series "The Darling Buds of May" and has since starred in the title role in the miniseries "Catherine the Great". Zeta Jones' stage credits include starring in London's West End production of "42nd Street".

Wilson has divided his time between distinguished stage and film projects. He starred in the psychological thriller "Death and the Maiden" and has also appeared in "The Age of Innocence," "Wetherby," "Lethal Weapon III" and "No Escape". Wilson is a graduate of England's Royal Academy of Dramatic Arts and began his professional career with the Royal Shakespeare Co., the Cambridge Repertory and the Liverpool Repertory. On television, he has appeared in the highly acclaimed PBS series "Prime Suspect," as well as "I Claudius, "Anna Karenina," "The Jewel in the Crown" and "Ivanhoe".

Matthew Letscher (Captain Love) recently starred in the television series "Almost Perfect". He has also appeared in the PBS American Playhouse presentation "Long Shadow" and numerous other series in guest-starring roles. He recently completed filming the movies "Friends of Friends" and "Power 98" for release later this year, and also appeared in the film "Gettysburg".

In addition to "GoldenEye," Campbell directed the 1994 film "No Escape". He also directed the award-winning BBC-TV thriller, "The Edge of Darkness," a five-hour presentation about nuclear contamination in England. The film garnered critical raves and six British Academy Awards.

Foster most recently produced the successful thriller "The River Wild," as well as the 1994 remake of "The Getaway". He also produced Sam Peckinpah's original, which starred Steve McQueen and Ali MacGraw. Among other films he has produced are "McCabe and Mrs. Miller," "The Drowning Pool," "The Mean Season," "The Thing," "Running Scared," "Heroes" and "Short Circuit I" and "II".

Claybourne has produced such films as "Jack," "Money Train," "D2: The Mighty Ducks," "Drop Zone," and "The War of the Roses". He began his career on Francis Ford Coppola's "Apocalypse Now" and went on to work with the acclaimed director as an assistant director on "The Black Stallion," producer of "The Escape Artist" and "Rumblefish," and as producer/assistant director of "The Black Stallion Returns". He also conceived and executive produced, with ZM Productions, the 1992 Ace and Emmy Award-winning documentary "Hearts of Darkness: A Filmmaker's Apocalypse".

Screenwriters Elliott and Rossio have collaborated on numerous screenplays, including "Robert A. Heinlein's The Puppet Masters" and "Little Monsters". They also co-wrote the hit animated film "Aladdin".

Eskow co-wrote "Air America" and "Above the Law," and wrote the Clint Eastwood comedy "Pink Cadillac".

Ward won an Academy Award for his screenplay of "The Sting". Additional screenplay credits include "Sleepless in Seattle," "Major League," which he also directed, and "The Milagro Beanfield War," which he adapted with the book's author, John Nichols
