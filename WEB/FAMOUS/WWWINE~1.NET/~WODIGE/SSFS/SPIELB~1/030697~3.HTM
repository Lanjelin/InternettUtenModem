March 6, 1997

Misfortunes on the 'Zorro' set

From Judith Sisson, co-ordinator of the Official Anthony Hopkins Fan Club:

Oz magazine carries two page spread titled "Latin in Black Satin" mainly about Antonio Banderas. It tells of the Zorro curse...bouts of flu in this case and tells how the production's first distant location in San Blas came to a complete halt.

Here are some extracts :

"Producer Steven Spielberg was called and told of the misfortunes, and the film's big stars Antonio and Sir Anthony Hopkins were told they couldn't leave Mexico. Luckily neither star came down with the flu, although Antonio developed strep throat and was unable to do some of his speaking parts......

The only person who was hanging in was Anthony Hopkins he was one of the few who remained in perfect health and in great spirits, despite his shaven head for the role of Don Diego. When asked how he did it Anthony replied 'I went to the doctor and was shot up with everything before I left'

Another source adds: 'The only thing he (Anthony) tried to avoid was the director...even though they are both Brits, things are just so-so between them. Tony is a pro and just wants to complete the role."

The spread contains no pictures of Tony (only Antonio and Melanie G)....however in "Last Word" section there is a small pic of him complete with shaven head, polaroids and blue sweatshirt...the caption reads... "Hair Raising : Anthony Hopkins is the chameleon of actors transforming his looks dramatically for such roles as Picasso, Nixon and Hannibal Lecter. Now his role in Zorro has gone to his head - perhaps he got too close to the masked man's slashing blade."
