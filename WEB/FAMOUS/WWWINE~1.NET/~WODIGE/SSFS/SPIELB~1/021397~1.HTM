February 13, 1997

Force feeds new 'Encounters'

From The Hollywood Reporter:

Columbia Pictures is preparing a theatrical rerelease this fall of its own 20-year-old science-fiction blockbuster, "Close Encounters of the Third Kind." It's one of several movies that studios are pulling out of their libraries and preparing for major new theatrical runs in the wake of the phenomenal success of the 20th anniversary special edition of "Star Wars," which grossed $70.8 million in its first 10 days of rerelease. Warners, which now controls the pre-1986 MGM library, is discussing a 30th anniversary release of "2001: A Space Odyssey" next year. Paramount, which has already announced a March 21 rerelease of "The Godfather," is also beefing up its previously planned rerelease of "Grease" next year for that film's 20th anniversary. Sources say Paramount is also seriously considering a 20th anniversary rerelease of "Saturday Night Fever" this year. (Scott Hettrick)
