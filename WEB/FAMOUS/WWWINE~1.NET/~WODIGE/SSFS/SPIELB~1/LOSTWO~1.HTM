PLOT:

It has been six years since Jurassic Park was destroyed, yet there are rumors that "something has survived." Dr. Ian Malcolm leads a team of scientists to investigate rumors that the genetically engineered dinosaurs are living on the park's secondary island and may have escaped to the mainland. Also, other biogenetic companies have heard about the technology which was utilized on Jurassic Park and are in search of DNA to create their own creatures.

DEVELOPMENTS - click on the date for full articles:

February 10, 1997: Merchandising information.

January 25, 1997: Production photo (above) from USA Today.

December 5, 1996: Christchurch newspaper "The Press" and Channel TV3 reported today that planned shooting of location scenes in New Zealand's Fiordland National Park for "The Lost World" will no longer take place because of changes in the production schedule. These scenes will now be shot in the USA, according to these reports. (News provided by Gary Whitcher).

October 7, 1996: Spielberg allows writer to helm film for a couple of days, under his supervision.

September 5, 1996: The Lost World begins shooting in Northern California.

August 10, 1996: Vince Vaughn joins the cast as a nature photographer. Filming begins Septemberg third.

August 7, 1996: Peter Stormare (Fargo ) may join the cast. Merchandising deals signed with Hasbro, DreamWorks Interactive, Fruit of the Loom, and Sega of America. Negotiations are underway with Burger King and Mercedes-Benz.
