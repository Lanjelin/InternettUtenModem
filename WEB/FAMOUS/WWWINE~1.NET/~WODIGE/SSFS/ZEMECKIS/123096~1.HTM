December 30, 1996

Coppola sues over Sagan's 'Contact' contract

From The Hollywood Reporter website:

Director Francis Ford Coppola's company has accused the late Carl Sagan and Warner Bros. of breaking a contract over the upcoming movie "Contact." The lawsuit, filed in Los Angeles Friday, asks that the movie production be stopped because, 20 years ago, Sagan had agreed to work with Coppola on a TV series based on the astronomer's 1985 book, "Contact." The suit alleges that Sagan later entered into a contract with Warner Bros. for the film. Sagan, a noted scientist who put astronomy into layman's terms, died Dec. 20. The plaintiff's lawyer, Robert Chapman, said Coppola and Sagan had agreed to share profits and any other projects related to "Contact." But Sagan went ahead with the Warner Bros. project without Coppola, Chapman said. (AP)
