February 25, 1997

'Jedi' pushed back: Fox makes room for 'Empire'

From the Hollywood Reporter:

The powerful performance of "The Empire Strikes Back: Special Edition" this weekend has set into motion a game of musical chairs in the marketplace. 20th Century Fox has decided, as expected, to push back by one week the release of the final chapter in the trilogy, "Return of the Jedi: Special Edition," in turn opening the door for Buena Vista to move up its Tim Allen comedy "Jungle 2 Jungle" into the spot vacated by "Jedi." "Jedi" will now go out March 14, providing a three-week window for "Empire" to fully exploit its engagements. "Empire" earned that right by returning $22 million during its first three days, good enough for the top spot at the weekend boxoffice. The highly anticipated "Jungle" was quickly moved into the March 7 slot, where it will oppose Paramount's Howard Stern comedy "Private Parts." This change makes sense in that the two features will not substantially compete for the same audience, with "Jungle" playing primarily to families and younger kids while "Parts" works the male adolescent market. (Roger Cels)
