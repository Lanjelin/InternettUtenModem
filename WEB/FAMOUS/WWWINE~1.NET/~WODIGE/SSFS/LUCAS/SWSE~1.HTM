Star Wars Trilogy:

Special Edition

Twentieth Century Fox/Lucasfilm Ltd.
{Star Wars | Empire Strikes Back | Return of the Jedi}


STAR WARS: EPISODE IV A NEW HOPE
Release Date: January 31, 1997


The rerelease of the original film comes during its twentieth anniversary. Industrial Light and Magic is restoring the actual original negative and improving sound quality for THX and digital sound. In order to make the film as close to George Lucas' original vision, some changes/additions are being made and a total of four minutes (about 75 shots) are being added:

  • The scene where Jabba the Hutt confronts Han at Mos Eisley will be added. A computer generated (CG) Jabba will interact with 20 year old film footage of Harrison Ford. Boba Fett also makes a guest appearance in this scene.
  • The Mos Eisley spaceport will look more like a spaceport. New computer generated creatures/aliens (a "ronto") will be added, in addition to more spaceships taking off and landing at the spaceport.
  • Digital effects showcasing the hovering effect of Luke's landspeeder will be added.
  • When the stormtroopers are on Tatooine, we will be able to see them on moving dewbacks (the large green creatures glipsed briefly in the 1977 version). There is also a CG stormtrooper which producer Rick McCallum says "will blow people away."
  • The matte lines around the spaceships in the end battle sequence will be removed.
  • According to Rick McCallum, there are also "other tiny little things that George has added that are really fun."


STAR WARS: EPISODE V THE EMPIRE STRIKES BACK
Release Date: February 21, 1997

The rerelease of this film will come six - twelve weeks after Star Wars. No new footage will be added.

  • Restored print, digital soundtrack in THX theaters.
  • Some shots in Cloud City will be recomposited.
  • RUMOR: More shots of the wampas in the ice cave. RUMOR


STAR WARS: EPISODE VI RETURN OF THE JEDI
Release Date: March 14, 1997

The rerelease of this film will come six-twelve weeks after Empire. One minute of new footage will be added. The only confirmed information is as follows:

  • Restored print, digital soundtrack in THX theaters.
  • Victory celebrations taking place on Coruscant (the Imperial Capital never before seen) and Tatooine, as well as Endor, will be added.
  • The explosion of the second Death Star will be, according to Steve Sansweet (a Lucasfilm representative) much more "chunkier."
  • Sy Snootles band will be redone in CG, as well as adding 9 new members to become a full 12 player "orchestra" with a bluesy-jazzy sort of music.
  • John Williams may be writing new portions of music.
    RUMOR: The Ewok celebration music will be changed.
