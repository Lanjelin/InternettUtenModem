February 18, 1997

'Star Wars' rereleasers lock onto global target

From the Hollywood Reporter:

Now that 20th Century Fox's rerelease of "Star Wars: The Special Edition" has exceeded all boxoffice projections in North America, Luke Skywalker and company are ready for global conquest. Commencing this spring, Fox is gearing up for a massive international "Star Wars" theatrical blitz in 57 territories that virtually mimics the U.S. marketing campaign. Not a kid or parent or potential toy buyer in the civilized world, it seems, will be ignored. As Fox International president Jim Gianopulos mused: "What's our demographic? The human race is our demographic." As successful as "Star Wars" was when it was first released, prospects are that the March rerelease will be as big a surprise internationally as it has been domestically. George Lucas' trilogy will greatly expand its universal appeal thanks to much-higher name recognition, improved media communication, greatly upgraded movie theater facilities and brand-new audiences that are galaxies apart from the world of exhibition in 1977. (James Ulmer and John Burman)
