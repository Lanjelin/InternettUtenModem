February 18, 1997

Star Wars Special Edition third weekend report

From the Hollywood Reporter:

'Star Wars' rules boxoffice galaxy
For the third consecutive weekend 20th Century Fox's rerelease of "Star Wars" has captured the top spot, beating back those who would usurp its throne with a dazzling estimated four-day gross of $21.8 million. Continuing to astound, "Star Wars" has now become the highest-grossing domestic film ever with an estimated $421.8 million in cumulative boxoffice for all releases. Along with "Star Wars," strong performances by wide releases helped power the four-day Presidents Day weekend to a record estimate in the $120 million area. Sony's "Absolute Power" flexed its muscle in the second spot with a strong estimate of $16.8 million for the four days. Universal's "Dante's Peak" held well in the third slot with an estimated $14.3 million, upping its total to an estimated $36.9 million. Warner Bros.' "Vegas Vacation" took its holiday in the fourth spot. The comedy, starring Chevy Chase and Beverly D'Angelo, took a gamble with an estimated $13.1 million for the four-day period Sony's romantic comedy "Fools Rush In," starring Matthew Perry and Salma Hayek, counted up an estimated $10 million for fifth. (Brian Fuson)
