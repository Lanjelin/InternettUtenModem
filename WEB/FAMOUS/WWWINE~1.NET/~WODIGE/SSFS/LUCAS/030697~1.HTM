March 6, 1997

Star Wars 5th Weekend, Empire 2nd weekend report

From the Hollywood Reporter:

'Brasco' plays against youth
The strong debut of Sony's "Donnie Brasco" and the degree of decline for 20th Century Fox's "The Empire Strikes Back: Special Edition" during its second session were the big stories at the boxoffice this past weekend. "Brasco" marshaled a mostly mature male customer into a muscular $11.7 million three-day debut to take No. 2, while No. 1 "Empire," with a boxoffice take of $13.1 million, remained a family-oriented enterprise augmented by sturdy teenage and young-adult attendance. Third place went to "Star Wars: Special Edition" with $6.6 million. The other new wide release for the weekend, Sony's "Booty Call," raised $6.4 million. Sony's "Absolute Power" continued to contribute, returning $5.7 million in its third weekend. (Roger Cels)

NOTE: "Empire" has now become the ninth-highest-grossing domestic boxoffice earner in history with an estimated cume for all releases of $262.4 million, just behind $263.7 million for Fox's "Return of the Jedi."

NOTE:"Star Wars" and "The Empire Strikes Back" together made up 44% of the total of the top 10 films for the month of February.
