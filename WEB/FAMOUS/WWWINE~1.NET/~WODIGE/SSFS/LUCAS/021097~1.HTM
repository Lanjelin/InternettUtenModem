February 10, 1997

Star Wars Special Edition second weekend report

From the Hollywood Reporter:

20th Century Fox's "Star Wars" cooled the boxoffice flow for Universal's big-budget "Dante's Peak" as the classic sci-fi adventure blazed to the top spot for the second consecutive weekend with a stellar estimate of $22.8 million, besting "Dante's" strong opening estimate of $18.6 million. The George Lucas-helmed reissue continues to defy naysayers as the film dropped a modest 36% from its opening weekend and has amassed a stunning total estimate of $69.3 million in 10 days. Its total domestic boxoffice estimate, including all reissues, now stands at $392 million. Other wide releases this weekend included Paramount's "The Beautician and the Beast," which debuted in the third slot with an estimated $4 million. Sony's long-legged "Jerry Maguire" held the fourth slot with an estimated $3.6 million. Dimension's "Scream" continued its successful run, as the Wes Craven-helmed thriller was fifth with an estimate of $3.5 million.
