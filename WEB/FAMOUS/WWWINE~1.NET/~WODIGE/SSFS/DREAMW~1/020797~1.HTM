February 7, 1997

Playa Vista wins financing

From The Hollywood Reporter:

More than $200 million has been committed by a group led by Los Angeles financier Gary Winnick to get the massive Playa Vista studio project back on track, officials confirmed Thursday. Maguire Thomas Partners, whose troubled multibillion-dollar project DreamWorks SKG hopes eventually to call home, said it is in negotiations to secure a final financing agreement. The announcement follows months of near-desperate activity on funding-related issues. Robert Maguire and James Thomas -- former partners in the development firm, which is now run by Maguire -- have juggled control of Playa Vista between them as they've worked with execs at DreamWorks to sort through a flap over who to pick as a funding partner and how to structure financing. Maguire Thomas spokeswoman Peggy Moretti said DreamWorks now is expected to move into Playa Vista sometime in 1999, or "two years from the time financing is secured." (Carl DiOrio)
