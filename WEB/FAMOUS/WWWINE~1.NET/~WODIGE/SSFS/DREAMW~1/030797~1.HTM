March 7, 1997

Mouse Hunt in production (full info)

From ClariNet Electronic News Service:

UNIVERSAL CITY, Calif.--(ENTERTAINMENT WIRE)--March 5, 1997-- Principal photography began March 3 on DreamWorks Pictures' comedy "Mouse Hunt," starring Nathan Lane and Lee Evans. The film is being shot in Los Angeles and Fresno, Calif.

Joining the cast in principal supporting roles are Vicki Lewis (`Beth' on NBC's "NewsRadio"), William Hickey ("Forget Paris," Academy Award nominee for "Prizzi's Honor"), Maury Chaykin ("Unstrung Heroes"), Michael Jeter ("Waterworld," CBS' "Evening Shade") and Eric Christmas ("Naked Gun 33 1/3").

In "Mouse Hunt," two hapless brothers, Ernie (Nathan Lane) and Lars Smuntz (Lee Evans) inherit a run-down house from their late father -- only to learn that the place is a historical mansion that is worth a fortune. The brothers are all set to fix up the house and cash in when they discover that there's someone else on the property who isn't so eager to sell: the world's smartest and meanest mouse who has no intention of relocating without a fight.

"We're very excited about `Mouse Hunt,'" stated DreamWorks feature executive Jason Hoffs. "It's a high-energy comedy with two terrific actors, Nathan Lane and Lee Evans, and a wonderful director, Gore Verbinski. We can't wait for audiences to see it. It should be a lot of fun for everyone."

"Mouse Hunt" is directed by first-time feature-film director Gore Verbinski, the Clio Award winner best known for his Nike "100 Foot Hoop" commercial featuring Michael Jordan, and the first in the popular Budweiser "Frogs" commercials.

The film is being produced by Alan Riche, Tony Ludwig and Bruce Cohen, with Bart Brown serving as associate producer. Executive producer is Mark Johnson. The original screenplay was written by Adam Rifkin ("The Dark Backward," ABC's "Bonechillers").

Producers Alan Riche & Tony Ludwig ("Empire Records") are partnered in Riche-Ludwig Productions and have a number of projects in development at various studios.

Bruce Cohen was the executive producer of the 1995 Universal release, "To Wong Foo, Thanks for Everything, Julie Newmar," and producer of the 1994 Universal blockbuster "The Flintstones."

Executive producer Mark Johnson is the Academy Award winning producer responsible for such films as "Rain Man" and "A Little Princess."

With his former partner Barry Levinson, Johnson produced or executive produced all of Levinson's films, beginning with "Diner" in 1982, followed by "The Natural," "Young Sherlock Holmes," "Tin Men," "Good Morning, Vietnam," "Rain Man" (which won four Academy Awards including Best Picture), "Avalon," "Bugsy," "Toys" and "Jimmy Hollywood."

His credits also include "A Perfect World" and the newly released dramatic thriller, "Donnie Brasco," starring Al Pacino and Johnny Depp.

The film will feature state-of-the-art visual effects from Rhythm & Hues Studios, the company that turned live animals into talking characters in the blockbuster "Babe," which earned them an Academy Award for Best Achievement in Visual Effects.

The special effects supervisor is five-time Oscar nominee and Academy Award winner for "Jurassic Park" Michael Lantieri ("The Lost World: Jurassic Park," "Casper," "Who Framed Roger Rabbit"). The animatronic creature design and effects are being created by Academy Award-winning Stan Winston Studio ("Jurassic Park").

Rounding out the outstanding behind-the-scenes team are director of photography Phedon Papamichael ("Phenomenon"), production designer Linda DeScenna ("Liar, Liar," five-time Oscar nominated set director), costume designer Jill Ohanneson ("Unstrung Heroes") and editor Craig Wood.

"Mouse Hunt" will be Nathan Lane's first film since the spring '96 smash "The Birdcage," which grossed in excess of $120 million domestically. He also starred in the Hallmark Hall of Fame production, "The Boys Next Door," for CBS, and hosted the 1994 and 1995 Tony Awards shows.

His other film credits include "Jeffrey," "Addams Family Values," "Frankie and Johnny" and "Life with Mikey." Lane is also widely known as the voice of `Timon,' the wise-cracking meercat of Walt Disney Pictures' blockbuster "The Lion King."

One of Broadway's more acclaimed and prolific performers, Lane recently finished his starring run in the revival of "A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Forum," for which he won the 1996 Best Actor in a Musical Tony Award, the Outer Critics Circle Award and the Drama Desk Award. He also received a Tony nomination for his work in the hit revival of "Guys & Dolls."

In addition to these accolades, Lane has just collected his first Daytime Emmy for Outstanding Performance in an Animated Program for his work in Disney's "The Lion King's Timon and Pumbaa."

Best known for his stage and television work, Lee Evans has headlined two British TV productions, "An Evening with Lee Evans" in 1993 and "The World of Lee Evans" in 1995. He made his feature-film debut in Peter Chelsom's critically acclaimed 1995 comedy "Funny Bones," co-starring Jerry Lewis, Oliver Platt and Leslie Caron.

He also starred as the Can Man in the 1995 Academy Award nominated short film, "Brooms." Evans will soon be seen in Luc Besson's "The Fifth Element," co-starring Bruce Willis and Gary Oldman.

Vicki Lewis plays Lee Evans' scheming and seductive wife, April Smuntz. Lewis can currently be seen as Beth, the offbeat sassy secretary on the hit NBC comedy "NewsRadio." A veteran of the New York stage, she appeared most recently in the Broadway revival of "Damn Yankees." Lewis' other television credits include a recurring role on "Home Improvement" as well as guest-starring roles on "Seinfeld," "Murphy Brown" and "Grace Under Fire."

William Hickey plays the brothers' father, string mogul Rudolph Smuntz. A veteran Hollywood character actor, Hickey received an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor as crime family boss Corrado Prizzi in John Huston's 1985 black-comedy "Prizzi's Honor." Most recently, he played Debra Winger's father in "Forget Paris."

His other film credits include "The Maddening," "Puppetmaster," "Pink Cadillac," "Da," "Bright Lights, Big City" and "The Name of the Rose."

"Mouse Hunt" will be distributed by DreamWorks SKG. DreamWorks SKG, a multifaceted entertainment studio, was formed in October 1994 by Steven Spielberg, Jeffrey Katzenberg and David Geffen.
