February 6, 1997

Dough for DreamWorks Playa Vista studio

From The Hollywood Reporter (sent in by Andrew Wallenstein):

HOLLYWOOD - It looks as if the dream's coming true for DreamWorks. An announcement is expected any day that a group led by Beverly Hills financier Gary Winnick will fork over $180 million to get its Playa del Rey project rolling. In exchange, Winnick's group - which also includes East Coast real estate and insurance interests - will get an ownership stake in the development that will have DreamWorks as its anchor tenant. Final approval of the financing arrangement by Robert Maguire, head of lead developer Maguire Thomas Partners, is said to be the only obstacle to announcing the deal. Maguire at least nominally was replaced at the project helm recently by his former partner James Thomas, with project decisions placed in the hands of a three-person panel. But an insider says Maguire's long resistance to equity participation by the Winnick group has stalled a final thumbs-up for the past several days.
