January 20, 1997

Bonnie Lee signs deal with DreamWorks

From the Hollywood Reporter:

Much-courted former Geffen Pictures executive Bonnie Lee disappeared from these pages shortly after the Geffen division merged into DreamWorks SKG. But now she's back. Sources say DreamWorks is soon to announce that Lee, who was wildly courted by Warner Bros. as well as the Steven Spielberg-David Geffen-Jeffrey Katzenberg organization, is about to announce that she's signed a producer deal with the studio. Among the projects she is already developing there is "The Deputy," based on a script by Kevin Quinn (who also wrote Universal's upcoming "Shadow of Death"). The picture, set in the Southwest, centers on a simple, noble sheriff's deputy who is assigned to protect a mobster in the witness protection program, then begins to form a complicated relationship with the man's wife. Lee, DreamWorks and Quinn's agent, Jim Stein of Writers & Artists, declined to comment.
