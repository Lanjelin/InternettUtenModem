February 19, 1997

Former ABC Executive Joins DreamWorks

From the Reuters - Variety Online Entertainment Report:

UNIVERSAL CITY, Calif. (Reuter) - DreamWorks SKG on Tuesday said it signed former ABC Inc. executive Ted Harbert to an exclusive deal to develop and produce television shows for the studio.

Harbert resigned his position as chairman of Walt Disney Co's ABC Entertainment on Feb. 15.

DreamWorks said Harbert would immediately join the situation comedy ``Arsenio'' as executive producer, along with Tim O'Donnell and Arsenio Hall. The show makes its debut on the ABC television network on Wednesday, March 5.

``To have a talent like Ted Harbert, who has 20 years of experience, go from the executive ranks to the producing side of the business is a unique and valuable opportunity,'' DreamWorks co-founder Jeffrey Katzenberg, said.

``He brings a tremendous wealth of experience and expertise to the table, and we are happy that he has decided to join us,'' he said

From The Hollywood Reporter:

Harbert makes sudden segue to DreamWorks
One work day after his last day as chairman of ABC Entertainment, Ted Harbert announced he has signed a 2 1/2-year exclusive deal to develop and produce TV shows for DreamWorks SKG. As part of the deal, Harbert will immediately become one of the executive producers of "Arsenio" -- DreamWorks' new sitcom for ABC -- along with Tim O'Donnell and star Arsenio Hall. That show premieres Wednesday, March 5. In January, when Harbert announced he was leaving ABC, he said he would take some time off before jumping into a new gig. "I had a long weekend," Harbert joked Tuesday. (Lisa de Moraes)
