February 18, 1997

DreamWorks goes 'Haunting'

From The Hollywood Reporter:

NEW YORK -- Looking beyond just a movie production, DreamWorks SKG paid several million dollars for the film rights to the nail-biting Shirley Jackson story, "The Haunting of Hill House." Following the mega-success of Universal's "Jurassic Park" and the high hopes for its sequel, "The Lost World: Jurassic Park," Steven Spielberg -- director of those films and a DreamWorks partner -- decided to put Computer Generated Imagery and marketing savvy to work for his own company. And quickly. The movie is already to skedded for a possible June production date. The movie centers on a haunted house and features a substantial number of supernatural occurrences that, in a '90s remake of 1963's "The Haunting," should lend themselves perfectly to the special effects that CGI now makes possible. (Joseph Steuer)
