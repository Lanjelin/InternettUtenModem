Luckman Interactive

Fans of the singer Suzanne Vega will most definitely want to stop at this outstanding home page. An extensive collection of interviews, biographies, profiles, and FAQs are included here, as well as Vega's own articles, essays, early poems, and bibliography. Listen to the collection of live and studio clips, or locate your favorite song in the complete library of her lyrics. Users can view images of album covers and publicity photos, or peruse the complete discography and music reviews.

YPN Music

This lovingly constructed site, centered around the politically conscious musician, includes audio clips, a discography, news on her album Nine Objects of Desire, profiles, articles, and concert reviews. The Vega fanzine Language is archived, and there are links to her favorite political crusaders, the folks at Amnesty International. In addition to her current writing, there's a sampling of Vega's early poetry.

Magellan

You couldn't really find much more extensive information on the female folk/alternative music star, Suzanne Vega, than at this page developed by Eric Szczerbinski and Hugo Westerlund. The site includes a complete discography of Vega's recordings and appearances including rare bootlegs from the mid-Eighties. Also included is a history of the artist's development including a detailed collection of lyrics and a rarely heard-about collection of the musician's early poetry dating back to 1969. The site provides links to the home page of the Vega fanzine 'Language' and to 'Undertow,'

inSITES iGUIDE

Excellent design, comprehensive coverage and an audio file "welcome" from the woman herself make this Suzanne Vega site a must-visit for fans. Also included are music clips, photo images, reviews and a discography.

NetHead Rock Site of The Day

A truly great site, with images, profiles, and interviews, along with a selection of Vega's own writing, including posts to the Net. Well-designed, with a wide and varied assortment of material.

Music Site of The Week

THE SUZANNE VEGA WEBSITE: Most of us remember Suzanne Vega for folk tunes such as "Marlene on the Wall" and the popular "Luka", however, in recent years Suzanne's musical direction has expanded to include industrial music, pop and rock. Her album, "99.9F", received wide critical success in America and abroad. Recently, she has released a new album titled; "Nine Objects of Desire". This album has received glowing praise from critics worldwide and is accompanied by the single "No Cheap Thrill", you can find them on A&M Records. The Suzanne Vega Web Site operated by Eric Szczerbinski, contains all the information and news you could ever need about Suzanne. Inside the tastfully designed pages you will find a complete discography, latest news, faq, bibliography, lyrics, articles, Suzannes early essays and poetry, a large photo gallery, sound clips, video clips, an email mailing list (on which Suzanne herself participates) and much more.

Cool Site Of The Week


I have seen many, many fan pages put together by individuals...and there are lots of great ones out there. So many in fact that I have never attempted to archive them. But The Suzanne Vega Website is one of the best I have seen. A complete and comprehensive site, this one includes cd and concert reviews, songs and poetry by the artist, pictures, and even a welcome message from Suzanne, herself.

Network Audio Bits E-zine

Are you aware that Soul Coughing's Ruby Vroom is named after the daughter (Ruby Maria Froom - b. July 8, 1994) of Suzanne Vega and Mitchell Froom? I wasn't until I browsed through some of Suzanne's comments/postings on the Suzanne Vega Website . The site contains a discography, scanned photographs, audio clips, and plenty of information. The inclusion of less-formal e-mail and postings/comments from Suzanne gives the site an informal, conversational feel. It's almost like having Suzanne over for a cup of tea and an afternoon chat. If you want to know about Suzanne Vega's projects, past, present and future, this is the place to look first.
