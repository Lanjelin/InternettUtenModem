Urban Survival: Applied Psychology and Methods.

Brief Description

This book is an outgrowth of the authors' background in martial art/self-defense instruction and their police experience. Urban Survival is a clear and concise how-to book containing numerous practical strategies to deal with urban crime. Topics treated include: obscene phone calls, rape, muggings, child safety, vehicle safety and domestic violence. A must for you self-defense library.

Bernd Weiss is a psychologist, reserve police officer and college professor. He is chief instructor of the nonprofit Topanga Karate School in California and has been practicing martial arts since 1955. He is a 7th degree black belt in Shotokan Karate. Hilda Weiss is a second degree black belt and has been practicing karate since 1975. Ms. Weiss holds a BA from UCLA in English Literature and a Masters in Business Administration. She is a reserve officer with the LAPD and has taught self-defense classes and held workshops in crime prevention.
