Workshop on Network Technology for Countries in the Early Stages of Internet Networking
15-22 June 1997
Permata Training Center (US $3,000)

This intensive, seven-day program emphasizes hands-on Internet set up, operations, maintenance, and management. Participants engage in extensive hands-on training, either setting up a prototype network or using actual Internet resources, using facilities at the Permata Training Center outside of Kuala Lumpur. The Workshop focuses on assisting countries that are either not yet connected to the Internet or are in the process of developing and enhancing an initial national Internet. The Workshop builds upon the success of workshops held during 1993-1996 at Stanford University, USA; Czech Technical University, Czech Republic; the University of Hawaii, USA; and McGill University, Canada.

K-12 Workshop:
Internet Society School Networking Colloquium
24 June 1997, Tuesday
Registration Fee: US $95 (early registration)/US $105 (late registration)

This one-day K-12 colloquium illuminates the broad range of technical and non-technical issues surrounding deployment and use of the Internet in schools, emphasizing primary and secondary education settings.
