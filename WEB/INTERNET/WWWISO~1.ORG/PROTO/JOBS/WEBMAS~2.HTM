Help Wanted

Webmaster Intern

The Internet Society’s International Secretariat, the international organization for global cooperation and coordination for the Internet and its internetworking technologies and applications, has an immediate requirement for a part-time on-site Webmaster for its Reston, Virginia, USA, headquarters office.

This could develop into a full-time summer internship.

We offer a friendly, small (but growing) office environment working for a prestigious Internet organization with a global, international focus.

We are looking for an individual with some college experience possessing the maturity and judgment that this position demands. You should have a good design sense, but, more importantly, must also possess a special sensitivity to content and how it may resonate with our diverse, multicultural, international audience. You must be a team player, flexible enough to assist with other systems-related tasks as necessary. The ideal candidate will be a college student possessing some formal training in graphics design, and will have some experience creating online or CD-ROM based multimedia presentations.

Applicants should list the operating systems, design software, and spoken and written languages (besides English) with which they are most familiar, including (but not limited to):

  • UNIX (e.g., Solaris, SunOS, Linux)
  • Graphics creation (e.g., Adobe Illustrator, Aldus Freehand)
  • Graphics manipulation (e.g., Adobe Photoshop)
  • Hypertext authoring (e.g., Adobe PageMill, HoTMetal, Netscape Navigator Gold)
  • Site maintenance (e.g., Adobe SiteMill)
  • Applications development (e.g., Macromedia Director, PERL, Java)
  • Database (e.g., Oracle, Sybase, Informix, SQL)
  • Language proficiencies (e.g., French, Spanish, German)

    Applicants should also be prepared to supply reference sites (when available) which they helped to create or maintain.

    The Internet Society offers a competitive intern rate working with leaders in the Internet industry. This is a friendly, non-smoking environment. Please, no phone calls.
