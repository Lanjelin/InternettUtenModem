I always say that a modern woman should feel free not only to break her own possessions, but to fix them, too. These are the most put-together sites on the web.


Home Central
The best feature here is an illustrated tool dictionary, plus lots of advice on dealing with headaches like old wiring and a leaky roof. See the divine site reviews, where Home Central calls The Faucet Outlet "one of the real stars of the online shopping world."

Toiletology 101
Kay Keating has been teaching toilet repair to women since 1973. She offers a complete course in fixing your toilet, plus advice on aesthetic repairs (changing the handle). The site is sponsored by a personal finance company -- they must be hoping for flush visitors.

Woman Motorist
Car expert Mary Jackson dropped out of graduate school 13 years ago to work in an auto shop and has been teaching other women about cars ever since. Here, her advice on basic maintenance, road safety, avoiding ripoffs and the other hazards of wheel life.
