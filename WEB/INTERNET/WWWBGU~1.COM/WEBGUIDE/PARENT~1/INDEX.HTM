Fed up? Want new kids? Nobody can help you with that, but here are some truly useful parenting sites.


Parent Soup
Parenting page par excellence -- nothing else comes close. A tremendous online community of parents committed to providing the answers, information, support and sanity vital to child-rearing. The hearty broth includes discussion groups, live chat, answers from experts and "Parents Picks" of toys, movies, vacations and software.

Parents at Home
Don't let the name fool you. Although this site is geared toward stay-at-home moms and dads, working parents will find plenty of information. Kids write to other kids and parents post messages to one another.

Parents Place.Com
This is the perfect site for support from folks who've "been there, done that," and for sharing your own insights. Topical live chats and an array of bulletin boards address everything from potty training and pre-teens to gay parents and gifted children. (P.S. Parents Place recently merged with Parent Soup, but they maintain separate web sites.)
