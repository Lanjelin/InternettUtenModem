Families with children will have a rewarding task when they visit Oslo and have to "find something the children will enjoy". We recommend in particular the amusement park Tusenfryd, a few kilometres south of Oslo.

Many of the best choices of entertainment for children are those offered by the large establishments: theatres, opera, cinema and museums.



Norgesparken Tusenfryd
N-1433 Vinterbro, Tel 64 94 63 63.
A classic amusement park. Round-abouts, big-dipper (with double-loop), water-dipper, bungy-jumping, flying carpet, stage show twice daily, restaurants, shops and many more (and new) attractions. Free bus from Oslo Central Station.

VikingLandet
N 1433 Vinterbro, Tel 64 94 63 63.
Norway's only theme park on the Vikings. Experience a Viking ship expedition, eat Viking food. Be a Viking.

Several circuses regularly visit Oslo. If you come here during summer you stand a good chance of a vist by clowns and elephants. Travelling fun fairs often stop by in Oslo as well, and set up their round abouts in one of the public squares. The circuses usually pitch their tents at Tøyen just by the Munch Museum and the Bothanical garden. Posters all over town warn of their coming.

Kids love a good puppet theatre, and Oslo has a special theatre dedicated entirely to this special art. It offers performances of high quality for kids aged 3 to 100!

Dukketeatret
Frognerveien 67,
N-0266 Oslo, Tel 22 42 11 88

Many of the city's regular theatres offer a bill for children as well: "Pippi Longstocking", "Oliver" and "Annie" can often be found on the bill. The Norwegian Opera also has productions for children. See "What's on?" and see the newspapers. And the Oslo cinemas always have several children's movies on their ply bill. Daily programme in all major newspapers.

The Norwegian Folk Museum
at Bygdøy has activities for children in the summer, children's exhibitions (for example, toys, dolls, art, horse-riding, milking, folk dancing).

Barnekunstmuseet (Museum of Children's Art)
is a unique museum for children with numerous "Workshops": drumming, singing, dancing, painting, music, drawing, drama etc.

Zoological Museum
in Tøyen is a paradise for children. Lectures, films.

For a fun tour of the city, try the Tourist Train Christian IV, departing regularly from Aker Brygge during summer.

All kids want to see Santa Claus. As we all know, he lives at the North Pole, but he can be reached by mail via his very own post office in Drøbak just outside of Oslo. Santa Claus in Drøbak answers letters sent by children all over the world and has his own Christmas postmark!

Julenissens Posthus
Torget, 1440 Drøbak
Tel 64 93 41 78. On the main Square in Drøbak.

Go Swimming!
In Oslo and the surrounding area you can find many beautiful beaches, as well as both inndoor and outdoor public swimming pools. Several of the little islands just a few minutes' boat ride from down town Oslo are pure gems for picknicks and swimming. At Hovedøya you can even explore the ruins of an old convent.
