 		Our publisher

CMP Media Inc. is a world leader in high-technology publishing, information and marketing services. Founded in 1971 by Gerry and Lilo Leeds, the company employs more than 1,500 people worldwide and has surpassed $381 million in annual sales. For 25 years, CMP has recognized that change is the key to innovation and growth in technology, and the company's ability to harness the forces of change has led to its own impressive growth. From its beginning with a single publication and a handful of editors, the company has grown to 14 publications with more than 250 editors in 18 bureaus worldwide. In 1995, CMP was tied as the largest high-technology publisher in terms of ad pages and market share in the United States, according to Toronto-based Inquiry Management Systems, an independent ad-tracking firm.

CMP has also evolved from a traditional print media company to a diversified provider of information and marketing services, ranging from database marketing, trade shows and conferences to CD-ROMs and online services. In the past three years the company has made a significant investment in the area of electronic media and in the fall of '94 became the first high-tech publishing company to launch all of its print products and business units on the Internet's World Wide Web, with CMP's TechWeb. By predicting change and moving quickly to capitalize on it, CMP has been able to achieve continuous growth averaging more than 20 percent per year, while maintaining its independence as a privately held company.

CMP also maintains a powerful presence in international markets with local-language publications. In 1994, CMP launched Informatiques Magazine, the only French-language monthly addressing the new computing paradigm in today's end-user companies. A German-lanuguage version of Computer Reseller News, CMP's highest-revenue domestic publication, launched in March 1995. CMP International also hosts a number of conferences and trade shows from Western Europe to Asia/Pacific. Take a look at CMP's Founding Principles.
