What are you ridin'?

he last bill from the auto shop did you in. You'd sooner walk to work than put another penny into that piece of ... junk. You know what you have to do, but you just can't face it. All the time you'll have to spend: Going to the library to read up on the new models. Asking your cousins how much they like their cars. Plowing through the hype in newspaper ads. Not to mention driving across town to several car dealers so you can have a series of schmoozefests with car salespeople. And that's just the beginning.

Wait up. It doesn't have to be that bad.

You'll find all kinds of resources on the Net to help you with every step of your car-owning experience, from buying a car and caring for it, to calculating your car insurance and car loan. You may even be able to pay your speeding tickets online. No more tedious treks to the DMV for you.

Think of it: You, at home, your feet up on the table, listening to your favorite band, eating pretzels, and buying a car.

Maybe you already have a decent car. At least you think your '91 Honda Civic is decent -- except for those strange noises coming from under the hood. Don't ignore those sounds anymore. Get some advice from a professional -- online. It's fast, it's easy, and hey, no one blames you for not wanting to spend your Saturday at Mick's Autobody.

-- Marieke Boer
