You call the shots

ure you like sports as much as the next person, but let's get real. Who has time to work an eight-hour day and rush home for a 5:30 p.m. Avalanche-Flyers faceoff?

You've fooled around with the Net enough to know that most sports info you want is there for the surfing. If you wanted to take the time to find the details, you could recite to your colleagues the stats of every player in the National Hockey League. But knowing Patrick Roy's (that's Rwah's) position in the 1982 draft and rattling off the details of his trade to Colorado on Dec. 6, 1995, isn't going to enhance your presentation to your boss this afternoon.

Wouldn't it be lovely if the sports scores you're after somehow appeared on your desktop? Wouldn't you be overjoyed to listen to play-by-play of your favorite team during your coffee break? What if you could actually watch the action online or be notified of an significant sports update?

Well, my friend, this magnificent technology does exist, and in the near future it will become as commonplace as the numbers that tell time in the corner of your monitor.

What's even better is that most of this information is practically hassle-free. No excruciating downloads, no interpreting techno-babble, and you don't have to submit your life history on a long registration form.

So roll up those sleeves. You have 10 minutes until lunch, just enough time to catch the latest scores or customize your very own sports feed.

Well, what are you waiting for?

-- Marieke Boer
