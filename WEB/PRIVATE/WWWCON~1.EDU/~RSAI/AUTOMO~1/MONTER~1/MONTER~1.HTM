The Monterey Historic Automobile Races, one of the most well known events for vintage racing enthusiasts, is held every fall at the Laguna Seca Raceway. Located only a few miles outside of Monterey California, the 2.238 mile track provides the perfect opportunity for owners and enthusiasts to experience the thrill and excitement of racing as it used to be. This year, over 40,000 attended the three days of qualifying and racing, a far cry from the 150 or so fans that were present during 1974, the first year the event was held.

Each year a different marque is featured and this year BMW took center stage. Many pre-war cars seldom seen elsewhere were on display including 315/1s, 327s, 328 Roadsters, 3.0CSs and more. Not only vintage cars were present, others included a 1995 BMW-powered McLaren F1 Le Mans car and the famous Art Cars, a series of BMW race cars painted by such famous artists as Andy Warhol, Frank Stella, Alexander Calder, and Roy Lichtenstein. Next year, the 24th consecutive year the event has been held, the featured marque will be Porsche.

For me, the most interesting part of the races is wandering the paddock area where you begin to get a real sense of the way racing used to be. Now a tradition long lost, at least in Formula 1, it was once possible to get an up-close look at the latest racing machines. It is at these type of events where enthusiasts once again get an opportunity to become acquainted with the cars and drivers. Watch the owners and mechanics prepare their cars, listen to the racing stories of veteran drivers, and feel the rumble of the engines. After strolling around the paddocks, it's time to climb the hill to get a great view of the infamous Corkscrew and a chance watch the actual races. There are fourteen different classes, everything from pre-war sports and Grand Prix racing cars to Can Am cars.
