Contes et légendes

  • Adapa le Sage (1991)
    Collections from the Department of Oriental Antiquities
    Inspired by a story from ancient Mesopotamia, which has come down to us in the form of fragments of three tablets dating from the IIth millennium B.C.


  • Chéops ou le secret des chambres de Thot (1991)
    Collections from the Department of Egyptian Antiquities
    Inspired by an incomplete papyrus known as the Westcar Papyrus, kept in Berlin, and dating from 1700-1600 B.C.
  • Saint François et le Loup (1991)
    Collections from the Department of Objets d'Art, the Department of Sculptures and the Department of Paintings
    Inspired by the lives of Saint Francis, including the Fioretti.
  • Babylone née des eaux (1990)
    Collections from the Department of Oriental Antiquities
    Inspired by the myth of the genesis.
  • Ce Coquin d'Hermès (1990)
    Collections from the Department of Greek, Etruscan and Roman Antiquities
    Inspired by a poem by Homer which retranscribes the most ancient myths.
  • Le Prince aux trois destins (1990)
    Collections from the Department of Egyptian Antiquities
    Inspired by a manuscript kept at the British Museum, the Harris 500 Papyrus, dating from the XIXth Dynasty (end of the reign of Seti I or beginning of that of Ramases II).
  • Les Aventures de Persée (1990)
    Collections from the Department of Greek, Etruscan and Roman Antiquities
    Inspired by a story from Greek mythology.
  • Un amour de porcelaine (1990)
    Collections from the Department of Objets d'Art
    Inspired by The Shepherdess and the Chimney Sweep by Andersen.
