HP ADVANCESTACK SWITCHING HUBS

EARN EDITOR'S CHOICE AWARD FOR PERFORMANCE AND FEATURES

Network Computing Ranks HP Above 3Com in Port-switching Comparison

PALO ALTO, Calif., Feb. 14, 1997 -- Hewlett-Packard Company, the leader in networked systems for workgroups, today announced that its AdvanceStack switching hubs were rated No. 1 in a comparison of port switches in the Feb. 1, 1997 issue of "Network Computing" magazine. HP received the Editor's Choice award over other port-switch manufacturers, including 3Com, LANart and Transition.

Port switching is a dynamic technology that allows ports to be moved, via software, to different segments on the network. The comparison evaluated port-switching hubs based on ease of use, configuration, price, overall features, scaleability and manageability. The HP AdvanceStack switching hub was the only product to score an "A" rating in Network Computing's product report card.

"This award recognizes HP's leadership in high-performance, easily managed networking," said Gary McAnally, general manager of HP's Workgroup Networks Division. "HP provides the best solution for upgrading from shared to switched environments. As end users require more bandwidth for Internet and intranet applications, HP AdvanceStack switching hubs provide an easy, low-cost way to keep up with demand for improved network performance."

HP's port-switching hub was the only product in the evaluation that could move nodes across network segments without using an additional external switch. HP's switching hubs also provide four additional switched ports for dedicated access to servers and network backbones.

The HP AdvanceStack switching hub is a 10Base-T hub capable of having ports assigned to any one of four internal segments. This feature provides four times the throughput found in single-segment 10Base-T hubs. The

HP AdvanceStack switching hubs come bundled with management and switch modules, enabling management and port-switching capabilities.

Automatic load-balancing was cited by the Network Computing article as one of the features that makes the HP AdvanceStack switching hub easy to configure and manage. This feature spreads network traffic automatically across all four segments in order to maximize network performance. Done with a simple click of a button, automatic load-balancing takes all the guesswork out of optimizing the network.

The Network Computing article awarding an Editor's Choice Award to the HP AdvanceStack switching hub can be found on the World Wide Web at http://www.nwc.com.

HP's Workgroup Network Division delivers a complete line of network workgroup solutions. Workgroup Network Division is a leading supplier of stackable hubs and high-speed networking products. Its products include AdvanceStack 10Base-T and 100VG hubs, AdvanceStack 10Base-T switching hubs, AdvanceStack 10/100Base-TX and 10/100VG switches, AdvanceStack routers and remote-access products and OpenView network-management applications.

Hewlett-Packard Company is a leading global manufacturer of computing, communications and measurement products and services recognized for excellence in quality and support. HP has 112,000 employees and had revenues of $38.4 billion in its 1996 fiscal year.
