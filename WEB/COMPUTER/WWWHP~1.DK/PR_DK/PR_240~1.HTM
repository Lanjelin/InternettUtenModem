HP NETSERVER SYSTEMS DISTINGUISHED IN 1996 WITH TOP HONORS AND CONTINUED RECOGNITION FROM KEY PC-SERVER-INDUSTRY INFLUENCERS


Leading Channel Publications and Industry Consultants
Also Recognize HP and its NetServer Systems


PALO ALTO, Calif., Jan. 22, 1997 -- Hewlett-Packard Company today announced that 1996 brought the company's HP NetServer systems more than 15 top honors from the computer industry's leading publications -- more awards than were received by key competitors Compaq and IBM combined. Honors were given across
the full PC-server line, from enterprise, to departmental, to self-supported-workgroup servers.


Highlights include the following:

o "Best Product of the Year" for file servers from PC Magazine for the HP NetServer LH systems (second consecutive year);

o "Best Products of the Year," MVP award for network servers from PC Computing for the innovative new HP NetServer E 30 system; and

o Best performance from Computerworld for the HP NetServer LX Pro.


In recognition of its strength among computer value-added resellers (VARs), dealers, integrators and throughout the channel, HP, with its NetServer systems, received for the third consecutive year the "Channel Champion Award" from Computer Reseller News. HP also received the highest marks from VARs in the "Annual Report Card" from VARBusiness.


In 1996, HP reached its goal for that year to be the No. 2 PC-server vendor, moving from No. 3 to No. 2 in the third-quarter PC-Server Results report by International Data Corporation (IDC).


"HP is very customer-focused and continues to design best-in-class solutions that are targeted to meet the specific needs of our customers and their markets," said Duncan Campbell, worldwide marketing manager of HP's Network Server Division. "It is clear from all these accolades that HP NetServer systems are hitting the target and that they are a PC server of choice with customers, in the channel and with top computer-industry influencers."


In its December 1996 issue, VARBusiness said, "In a near clean sweep, HP wins over Digital, Compaq and IBM with seven out of eight top scores." PC Magazine said, "With excellent design, performance, functionality and a mature management solution in its NetServer Navigator, the HP NetServer 5/133 LH is a network administrator's dream."


Computerworld said, "HP designed its NetServer LX Pro with attention to the details necessary to enable ready management and configuration of the server." PC World said, "Once again, an HP server proves a super performer."


In addition to the numerous awards and honors bestowed on HP NetServer systems last year, the servers also repeatedly won the PC-server product-review roundups conducted by leading computing publications.

In 1996, HP extended its award-winning NetServer PC-server line to include Pentium Pro in its midrange and high-end systems and introduced the HP NetServer E Series, which was created specifically for self-supported workgroups.


HP's complete family of NetServer systems consists of two distinct lines of PC servers, as well as a fully pre-configured Internet solution available in North America, the HP NetServer Web Master. The HP NetServer E 30 is the first of the E Series servers designed for small to midsized businesses or remote corporate sites with little or no MIS support. The award-winning L Series spans from the entry-level HP NetServer LD Pro corporate workgroup server to the departmental HP NetServer LH servers through the enterprise-level HP NetServer LXe and LX Pro application servers. All HP NetServers come equipped with an HP NetServer Navigator CD-ROM, which features easy-to-use tools to configure, install and manage the servers.


Customer-voted, Award-winning, Worldwide Service and Support

HP's L Series servers are backed by a standard three-year, next-day, limited warranty, which is available from HP service technicians or authorized resellers. HP NetServer systems also are covered by a wide range of HP SupportPack services, which provide three-year same-day or 24 X 7 hardware support, server installation and network configuration, and network operating-system support. HP SupportPack services are sold by resellers and delivered by HP.


Hewlett-Packard Company is a leading global manufacturer of computing, communications and measurement products and services recognized for excellence in quality and support. HP has 112,000 employees and had revenues of $38.4 billion in its 1996 fiscal year.
