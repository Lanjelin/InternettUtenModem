Fractal Information at Other Sites

  • Dick Oliver dick.oliver@netletter.com presents Non-linear Nonsense. An online newsletter dedicated to describing current trends in Fractals and how they are being applied in todays world.
  • Steve Finch at mathsoft sfinch@mathsoft.com let me know of a document discussing Traveling Salesman Constants which give rise to some very familiar fractal structure.
  • Ronnie Mainieri ronnie@cnls.lanl.gov has a webpage designed for Bibliography searches in the world of Chaos.
  • A list of Weblinks to Fractals and related info from bengtmn@algonet.se
  • Iterated Systems will provide a Fractal Plug-in for Netscape To View their FIF "Fractal Image Format" files.
  • The Wonder-World of Mandelbrot created by Zsolt Zsoldos zsolt@mi.leeds.ac.uk contains an introduction to the mandelbrot along with a family of images, as well as a collection of links to a variety of fractal material.
  • Professor Ken Musgrave musgrave@seas.gwu.edu has a very interesting webpage titled Building Fractal Planets and it is well worth browsing if your casually interested and there is material and reference for detailed study for the serious student.
  • The latest Sci.fractals Faq is now available in html format from Oxford University Libraries WWW server.
  • A list of links to Chaos and Fractals from hartmut@mathematik.uni-Bremen.de
  • The Yoshikawa Lab, in Japan provides an index to a Nonlinear WWW server list .
  • Doug Martin dmartin@ealnet.com has produced a highschool and college level introduction to fractals called Fract-Ed and it is available for your evaluation.
  • Frode Gill in Norway, fgill@adh.no created two introductory Web pages on fractals. The first one is An Introduction to Mandelbrot programming and the other page is An Introduction to Quaternion Julia sets
  • Wentian Li of Rockefeller University wli@linkage.rockefeller.edu has created a webpage dedicated to Papers on 1/f noise
  • Prof. A.J. Roberts homepage at the Mathematics & Computing department of the University of Southern Queensland has some interesting papers on Modelling Dynamical Systems
  • An introduction to Dynamical Systems and Fractal Geometry from Robert L. Devany and the Dept. of Mathematics at Boston University. He also presents an interesting series of pages called Chaos in the Classroom.
  • Joergen K. Kanters jkanters@mfi.ku.dk maintains a short list of people and web pages for people studying chaos in cardio-vascular research titled Chaos and the Heart in the Web.
  • Claus-Dieter Schulz gives us The Fractal Music Project from Univ. of Stuttgart.
  • Look at an intro to a course called "Introduction to Fractal Geometry from rajiyer@seas.upenn.edu
  • The Institute of Computer Graphics at the Technical University of Vienna present some information on Nonlinear Iterated Function Systems.
  • A good online document on Feigenbaum Constants is now available along with many other mathematical constants from the mathsoft website http://www.mathsoft.com/ .
  • The Chaos and Complexity Homepage from Democritus University of Thrace.
  • An introduction to fractals titled "Exploring Chaos and Fractals" from millbrook.lib.rmit.edu.au.
  • The www site of the Fractal Research Initiative. A collaborative effort from groups at Waterloo-Montreal-Rocquencourt.
  • An "Introduction to Fractals from Paul Bourke in Auckland New Zealand. He also has a wealth of useful and instructive fractal information on his fractal page.
  • J.C. Sprotts Fractal Gallery from University of Wisconsin.
  • The "Chaos Metalink", from Industrial Street Productions is a comprehensive list of links to the world of chaos and fractals.
  • A quick review of Research Interests in fractals and Chaos from University of Maryland.
  • A very informative "Fractal Gallery from Alberto Strumia in Italy.
  • A bibliography of papers on "Fractal Compression" from Brendt Wohlberg brendt@dip.ee.uct.ac.za
  • You can ftp Another bibliography on "Fractal Compression" from Univ Waterloo in Ontario, Canada.
  • A html paper on fractal compression entitled "The Bath Fractal Transform" From Bath University in the UK.
  • An interesting collection of work with fractals from "Groupe Fractales" at INRIA in France.
  • Michael L. Richardson, M. D. and Thurman Gillespy III, M. D. present a paper on "Fractal Analysis of Trabecular Bone "
  • A reference to "Fractal Clouds" from cahalan@clouds.gsfc.nasa.gov
  • P.J.Drinkwater at Loughborough University of Technology presents a 3 Dim. L-system Tree program and some images.
  • Stanford University points to some fractal material.
  • A list of Publications on Fractal Image Compression and Related material from brendt@dip1.ee.uct.ac.za.
  • The Faculty page listing of Benoit B. Mandelbrot at Yale University.
  • The Fractal Microscope at University of Illinois at Urbana Champaign
  • The Australian National University (ANU) database for Complex Systems.
  • Yuval Fisher's WWW site for information on Fractal Compression at inls.ucsd.edu
  • Michael Barnsley's WWW site for Iterated Systems Inc. a commercial fractal compression company.
  • Some papers on fractal compression from Dietmar Saupe and Raouf Hamzaoui at University of Freiburg
  • The IMS preprints FTP archive at math.sunysb.edu
  • Dave Sag dave@va.com.au in Australia has updated his fractal music pages titled Experiments with fractal music. He now incorporates real audio.

Note

This collection of pointers is constantly growing and changing. I try to keep all the information current and the links accurate and up-to-date. The nature of the Web makes this a difficult task. Many pages have a short life, or service is intermittent or switched to another server. If you find a reference here that is consistently unavailable for an extended period of time, or just plain wrong, please email me and I will either correct it if I can, or remove it. Also, if you have created a new page with some interesting fractal information and want to have it posted here, I will gladly make an insertion to the list.
