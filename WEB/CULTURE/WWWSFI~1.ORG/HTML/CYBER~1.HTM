INTERNET RELAY CHAT (IRC)
  • #starfleet -- for those who wish to chat in Real Time, we have the 'official' STARFLEET IRC channel. Here are the instructions, courtesy of Michael Smith (msmith@cyberwar.com):

    We use the chat service called XWorld. To access use:

    kansas-city.ks.us.x-world.org (irc.lips.net)
    pensacola.fl.us.x-world.org (sunkist.lod.com)
    Larcenybay.CA.US.XWorld.Org (irc.hackintosh.com)
    buffalo.NY.US.XWorld.Org (IRC.BlueMoon.Net)
    Los-Angeles.CA.US.XWorld.Org (irc.moogle.org)
    Marietta.OH.US.XWorld.Org (irc.punx.net)
    Providence.RI.US.XWorld.Org (XWorld.InfoAve.Net)
    RockHill.SC.US.XWorld.Org (XWorld.InfoAve.Net)
    Telford.UK.EU.XWorld.Org (irc.Enta.Net)
    Waco.TX.US.XWorld.Org (Utopia.Cord.Org)
    Wesel.DE.EU.XWorld.Org (IRC.StarTrek.NDRH.De)

    All connections are port 6667

    Per the XWorld admin, use the default server name (the one in parentheses) and set your connect time (if available to 45-60 seconds).

    Then from there, just type
    /join #starfleet

    Fleet Admiral Michael Smith will hold monthly "Fireside IRC Chats" to be held during the week of each month. The next will be Thursday, 27 March starting at 9pm eastern time (6pm pacific time). For more information, please contact the Moderator, Allyson M.W. Dyar (NetServices@sfi.org)

    View the log of the first chat Wednesday, 26 February

    For a list of Frequently Used Nicknames (nicks), please consult STARFLEET Internet Nick List

    While STARFLEET can't officially endorse any specific IRC client, the majority of those who access IRC and have a PC use mIRC. The advantage of using mIRC is that if you run into problems, there is likely to be someone available on or off IRC who can help you set it up.

  • #starfleet-kansas_sector -- was established for the Kansas chapters to get together and exchange dates and activities, but anyone is welcome. For more information, contact Michael W. Malotte (nomadco@smartnet.net)

 

GENERAL MEMBERSHIP LIST SERVERS

  • starfleet@netrail.net is an independent List Server that serves as STARFLEET's main conduit of general information and natter. We discuss STARFLEET related topics and the odd comment about Star Trek is occasionally seen, however the main topic of discussion is STARFLEET. For information on how to subscribe to the list, please access the List FAQ or E-mail the List's Administrator Lisa N Paradis Berkenbilt (paradis@netrail.net) for more information. You can also view the more recent ListServer messages via hypertext.

  • sfi-announce@sfi.org is the official announcements-only listserver for those who want to keep up with important 'fleet related information but without the additional commentary and traffic on the STARFLEET List Server (info above). This list is administered by Allyson M.W. Dyar (NetServices@sfi.org)

    To subscribe: send a message to listserver@sfi.org
    and in the body of the message put subscribe sfi-announce your name
    for example: subscribe sfi-announce Michael Q Fleeter.
    You can also view the an abbreviated version of announcements and download archives.

    For more information on the Announcements ListServer, please consult the Announcements FAQ.

 

USENET NEWS GROUPS -- alt.org.starfleet is our USENET group, set up to spread the word about STARFLEET and our activities. The point of contact for the group is Allyson M.W. Dyar (NetServices@sfi.org).
