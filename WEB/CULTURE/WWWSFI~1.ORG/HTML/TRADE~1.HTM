Gaming

  • The Continuum OnLine is a Star Trek role-playing club that features *Email Sims* in addition to interactive chat room sims. Role-playing at its best! If you're interested in joining, or would like more information, please contact us via e-mail (TheTigress@aol.com)

  • STF is a great e-mail role play simulation adventure for any Star Trek fan! Nowhere else can you be the 24th century person you always wished you could be, try out new ideas that you can't try in real life, meet interesting people, write science fiction, and have a blast all at the same time! ... it's the best way to find your creative nitch within the realm of the great Universe of Cyberspace. It is the begining of multiple great beginings, and the opportunities are endless! Our e-mail simms are dedicated to people who don't have the time to spend a large amount of time online, but that people check their mail and reply at their leisure. Contact join by e-mail (FANottage@aol.com) for more information.

  • Interested in Role Playing in the TNG universe, then link to a Star Trek RPG by the United Federation of Players or join by e-mail (joinufp@umich.edu).

  • United Alliance of Planet Simulations is a role-playing organization that meets for an hour a week to play out various positions on a futuristic ship. Our sims are based on Star Trek. Most of our sims take place in the year 2396, therefore we use state of the art technology, not even used in the current Star Trek series. For more information, please contact the UAP Coordinator for more information.

Groups/Organizations

  • Alpha Quadrant is a national (soon to be international)service organization whose members are Star Trek and science-fiction enthusiasts. AQ members feel that the open-minded, progressive society represented in Star Trek is a worthy goal for humanity, and perhaps, our ultimate destination.

  • The Doctor's Exchange is the authorized fan club of Siddig El Fadil/Alexander Siddig, who portrays Dr. Julian Bashir on Star Trek: Deep Space Nine. Membership in the club includes an autographed photo, membership packet (including biography), and a subscription to the club newsletter, Multi-Species Medicine. The club also has an annual meeting, which raises funds for the club charity, Amnesty International. Membership is $25 in North America, $35 elsewhere (U.S. funds). Make check payable to Joan Marie Verba, P. O. Box 1363, Minnetonka, MN 55345-0363. For further information, Joan Marie Verba (verba001@maroon.tc.umn.edu)

  • The Federation or e-mail Chris Cancilla (cancilla@mont.mindspring.com)

  • The USS Harvey Milk Gay & Lesbian Star Trek Association is a cyberspace organization created by and for gay and lesbians fans interested in Star Trek.

  • International Federation of Trekkers is a fan club designed to promote the humanistic philosophies and ideals portrayed in the Star Trek mythos in a realistic fashion conducive to the continued existence and responsible advancement of human kind. To provide, promote, and support education and positive legal activism in the area of human rights, racial and sexual equality, environmental causes, and space exploration. To promote a more positive impression of Star Trek enthusiasts as intelligent, socially conscious, active individuals who are aware and concerned about the real world today.

  • Klingon Assault Group Home Page . Are the Feddies too soft for you? Wanna hang with real warriors? Then KAG is for you. And tell 'em the good guys sent you!

  • The Klingon Imperial Diplomatic Corps (KIDC) is a non-profit international organization dedicated to the promotion and fostering of Klingon culture, language and society here on Earth.

  • The Klingon Language Institute is dedicated to the study and support of the Klingon language. It offers a free postal course, a quarterly journal, as well as translation projects including the works of Shakespeare and Hamlet. With more than 1000 members across over 30 countries, the KLI is spreading Klingon around the world.

  • Matrix Task Force was established to provide an internet Fan club support group that strives for cooperation, communication, friendship and fun regardless of organizational association. To provide a forum through which independant chapters and affiliated chapters may benefit from each others experience, and offer services to Task Force chapters and Internet Fandom as a whole. For more information e-mail Roger Stearns (pstearns@comp.uark.edu)

  • Patrick Stewart Appreciation Society (PSAS) the UK-based fanclub authorised by actor Patrick Stewart, of Star Trek and Royal Shakepseare Company fame.

  • Romulan International Empire -- The R.I.E. is the 3+ year old fan organization whose members are united the world over in their appreciation of Star Trek, and the race (ROMULANS). Dozens of chapters worldwide link members into local fandom as well as the International organization.

  • Looking for other Romulans? Then check out the Home Page of the Romulan Star Empire. Or e-mail Romulan Star Empire Info (rse@homeworld.com)

  • Space: Above and Beyond Internet Directory/Yellow Pages -- All things SAaB!

  • Starfleet International Home Page. From the wonderful folks who bring you this Trading Post page...!

  • Starfleet Marines International Web Page or e-mail Rafi Sharif for more information.

  • The Star Trek Welcommittee is the longest-running Star Trek fan organization dedicated to answering questions on Star Trek and Star Trek Fandom.

  • UFPI -- United Federational of Planets Internationale Home Page.

  • Star Trek International - An International Fan Club with a devotion to its fans with a general theme of Star Trek, but also exploring the other realms of Sci-Fi. Star Trek International is based upon three guiding principles - Freedom, Honor, & Unity. Be a part of the fans, boldly exploring the realms of fandom and join STI Today! For more information, please email the Commander - Star Trek International at Adam Day or click here for an for membership application.

Publications

  • The Communique OnLine is a newsletter/E-zine primarily dedicated towards those who are participating in the "simulations" that are run on America OnLine, or even the newer "real time" simulations on the IRC, but there are articles that will be of interest to any and all Trekkies/Trekkers! Visit our Web Page or contact the Editor-in-Chief (TheTigress@aol.com) via e-mail.

  • Dateline: Starfleet. This is a collection of all kinds of interesting stuff about Star Trek and is distributed every mid-month to interested fans on the Internet. The Editor is Bill Mason (data1701d@aol.com). Drop him a line for further info.

  • Federation Chronicles -- A quarterly Star Trek fan fiction magazine.

  • Bjo Trimble's Sci-Fi Spotlite online highly regarded publication on media fandom edited by the woman who "saved Star Trek" back in the 60s.

  • Science Fiction Weekly a new professional electronic publication of science fiction news, reviews and short stories. Visit them at their Web Page.

  • TOS -- A new letterzine with a focus on Classic Trek is in the stages of being formed. This zine is an opportunity for those fans who love The Original Series to reminisce, reflect and comment upon it, and share their opinions with others. For more information, contact Nora Mayers (n.mayers@genie.geis.com)

Star Trek related Web Sites

  • Fandom Directory is the largest single publication devoted to All Things Fannish. The paper publication itself is highly recommended and the web site is awesome!

  • Luca Sambucci's Site -- the 'motherload' of Star Trek sites. If it's not here, it doesn't exist in the Trek universe!

  • Marcus E. Hennecke's Links to Star Trek Sites. As he puts it on his web page: "I also hate pages that don't provide any information and only point to other sites. But since others have already done an excellent job collecting interesting facts, why should I reinvent the wheel?" It's rather a keen page and nice if you don't like a lot of graphics.

  • Official Star Trek 30th Anniversary Web Site -- All things officially Paramount's Star Trek; 'nuff said.

  • Science Fiction Resource Guide is a page contains an extensive database of science fiction information which can be accessed by hyperlinks. Subject areas include archives, authors, bibliographies and lists, movies, bookstores, role-playing games, zines, etc. Some of the topics include Anne Rice, Bram Stoker, Jules Verne, H G Wells, Star Wars, Star Trek, nanotechnology, vampire fiction, and more.

  • The Sci-Fi Channel. This really needs no introduction, except to check it out! They list the schedule, special offers to Web Surfers and all kinds of good stuff.

  • TOS WWW Services Site represents the largest WWW site on the Internet dedicated EXCLUSIVELY to the Original Series, Movies, and Books. Try out TOS Trek a multi-user roleplaying environment for those who love the Original Crew. You can also telnet directly to TOS Trek And if all else fails, you can contact the webmaster via e-mail (macgyver@tos.net)

  • TrekNet is a fan-run computer network covering every aspect of the Star Trek universe and other science fiction and fantasy topics. We currently have members or representatives of many different Star Trek fan clubs online with Treknet (tm), including KAG, KLAW, Starfleet Marines, Starfleet International, The Federation, Starfleet Command, Starfleet Rangers, United Federation of Planets Internationale, Star Trek Welcommittee, the BSTA and many, many local groups!
