WHERE TO TREK IN THE KINGDOM

The entire length and breath of the Kingdom is a paradise for trekkers. There are easy walks lasting a few days and there are strenuous expeditions that takes several weeks. All of them have great scenery. Here are few regions we offer to you:

ANNAPURNA 4 TO 26 DAYS

Grade: Easy/Strenuous |
Group size: Minimum 2

The most popular trekking route in the kingdom of Nepal takes you through some of the most stunning scenery on the earth. You will be walking through rhododendron, pine and bamboo forests over the foothills of Annapurna, Dhaulagiri ranges and through the Kaligandaki gorges, the deepest in the world.

EVEREST 10 T0 32 DAYS

Grade: Easy/Strenuous
Group Size: Minimum 2

The highest mountain in the world continuous to lure adventurers as ever. You can walk through the famous sherpas villages to Namche Bazar and on to Tengboche monastery with the mesmerizing peak of Amadablam hovering in the sky. Then it's over the glaciers to the foot of the Mt. Everest, the highest mountain in the world for the view of a life time.

LANGTANG 10 T0 18 DAYS

Grade: Easy/Strenuous
Group size: Minimum 2

The third most popular trekking route in the kingdom of Nepal lies directly north of Kathmandu. The Gosainkund lakes situated at altitudes of over 4000 m. here are a much revered Hindu pilgrimage site. Langtang offers you baffling extremes in topography and climate conditions. In just one day you can traverse five different vegetation zones trekking from the subtropics to timberline.

DOLPO 12 TO 26 DAYS

Grade: Strenuous/Rigorous
Group Size: Minimum 4

The Dolpo is located inside the Shey-Phoksindo National Park of mid western Nepal, behind the Dhaulagiri massif towards the Tibetan plateau. Cut off by a series of very high passes, closed by snow most of the year. Dolpo remains a truly isolated corner of Nepal.

MUSTANG 15 TO 21 DAYS

Group Size: Minimum 4
Grade: Rigorous

This is the most popular and fascinating trekking route in the kingdom of Nepal. The H.M.G. of Nepal opened the door to this area for trekkers in 1991 under strict control on obtaining a special permit for this region. The strictness is enforced to protect the tradition and as well as the environment of this region.

KANCHENJUNGA 18 TO 28 DAYS

Grade: Strenuous
Group size: Minimum 4

The massive Kanchenjunga (8598 m.) lies on the border of Nepal and Sikkim. It is a rich area of the Kingdom for musk deer, Blue Sheep. It also offers excellent photographic views of Everest, Makalu and Kanchenjunga itself. It is inhabited by few scattered population and is a real wilderness terrain of the Kingdom.

MANASALU 22 TO 26 DAYS

Grade: Rigorous
Group size: Minimum 4

This route of trekking was forbidden to the tourists upto 1991, later on when the Japanese expedition team ascended the Manasalu peak (8157 m.), it become popular as " Japanese mountain." This trek begins from Gorkha or Trisuli.

MAKALU 18 T0 28 DAYS

Grade: Strenuous
Group size: Minimum 4

Makaku at 8481 m., is the 5th highest mountain in the world and lies on the Nepal-Tibet border. The extensive views of all Nepal 's major peaks to the East of Everest are the main highlights of this difficult and fascinating trek through seldom traveled terrain.

GANESH HIMAL 16 TO 21 DAYS

Grade: Strenuous
Group size: Minimum 4

There are several major groups of peaks: Ganesh Himal, Langtang Lirung, Manaslu, Himal Chuli and the large Annapurna Himal.This trek is recommended by many trekkers. It is famous for Tamang culture, spectacular views of all mountains, rhododendron forest.

PANCHASE TREK 6 TO 10 DAYS

Grade: Easy
Group size: Minimum 4

As one is walking along the peaceful ranges with terraced hills all round, colourful fauna painting the landscape, eagles soaring high above, and the majestic Annapurna Himalayas towering all round, one can feel higher than royalty, discovering the nature height of being alive.
