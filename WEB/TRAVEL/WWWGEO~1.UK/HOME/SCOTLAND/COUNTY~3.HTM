Administrative Divisions in Scotland after 1889

Under the Local Government (Scotland) Act, 1889 many changes were made in the county boundaries, detached portions of Nairn, Perth, Selkirk & etc. being annexed to the counties surrounding them; whilst Orkney and Shetland, united for parliamentary purposes, were separated.

Thus there were 33 counties after 1889:

  • Aberdeen
  • Argyll
  • Banff
  • Berwick
  • Bute
  • Caithness
  • Clackmannan
  • Dumbarton
  • Dumfries
  • Edinburgh
  • Elgin
  • Fife
  • Forfar
  • Haddington
  • Inverness
  • Kincardine
  • Kinross
  • Kirkcudbright
  • Lanark
  • Linlithgow
  • Nairn
  • Orkney
  • Peebles
  • Perth
  • Renfrew
  • Ross and Cromarty
  • Roxburgh
  • Selkirk
  • Shetland
  • Stirling
  • Sutherland
  • Wigtown
