Royal Scottish Geographical Society

Photographic Competition

Each year RSGS runs a photographic competition for members. There are prizes of photographic equipment and the winning photographs from each of the three categories are published in Geogscot.

Each of the RSGS Regional Centres runs its own competition, the winners of which go forward to the national competition.

The 1996 first prize winner is Anne Jenkins of the Glasgow Centre with her dramatic image of the crater of Mount Etna. She wins a camera donated by Olympus. The runner-up, David Robertson of Stirling, wins a camera bag donated by Billingham Bags.

The 1997 photographic competition will be judged in November.
