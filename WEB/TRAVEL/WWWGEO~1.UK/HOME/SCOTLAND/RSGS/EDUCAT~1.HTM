Royal Scottish Geographical Society

Education

The RSGS was founded in 1884 as an educational trust and education is a fundamental remit of the Society.

Through lectures, symposia, education packs, support for geography teaching in Scotland's schools and universities, and through close liaison with government, the Society promotes the quality and growth of geographical and environmental education in Scotland.

The RSGS Medal for Best Graduating Student in Geography

The Society's medal was won in 1996 by Roderick Ferguson of the University of Glasgow.

The RSGS - John Bartholomew Essay Competition

The competition for 1996 attracted over 140 entries from school pupils. The twelve prize winners were presented with atlases at the City Hall, Glasgow.
