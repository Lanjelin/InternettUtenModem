Chapter 23

Useful Travel Information


1. Weights and Measures

  • One English stone is 14 pounds.
  • One kilogram is about 2.2 pounds.
  • A 155 pound person weighs 70 kilograms or 11 stone.
  • One ounce is about 28½ grams. At a deli you might ask for 100 or 200 grams of cheese, which is about 3½ or 7 ounces.
  • One gram weighs about the same as one regular paper clip.

  • One liter is about 2 ounces more than one quart.

  • One inch is 2.54 centimeters.
  • One millimeter is about the width of a dime edge.
  • One meter is 3 inches more than one yard.
  • 12 inches are one foot.
  • 3 feet are one yard.
  • A person 5' 10" tall is 1.75 meters (175 centimeters).

  • One hectare is 100 meters by 100 meters, or about 2½ acres.

  • One kilometer is about 6/10 of one mile.
  • 100 kilometers per hour is about 60 miles per hour.
  • A mile is 5280 feet or 1760 yards.

  • Freezing is 0° centigrade (same as Celsius) or 32° Fahrenheit.
  • Boiling is 100° c. or 212° F.
  • 0° F. is -18° c.
  • 100° F. is 38° c.
  • 75° F. is 25° c., or in Ireland a killer heat wave with children and old people collapsing on the streets.
