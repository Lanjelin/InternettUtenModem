Disaster Services

When Help Can't Wait in the Fight Against HIV / AIDS:


HIV / AIDS Education


Today, AIDS is the leading cause of death among Americans aged 25 to 44. There is no cure.

But by learning the facts and acting on them, we can protect ourselves and others. You can help. Become a Red Cross HIV/AIDS instructor or host an HIV/AIDS educational session in your community.

The American Red Cross, a leader wtih proven effectiveness in educating people about HIV and AIDS for over 10 years, has a new weapon in the fight against the spread of HIV. It's an educational program titled Basic HIV/AIDS Program:Fundamentals, which uses a variety of learning activities to help people:

  • Respond in informed ways to the risk of HIV invection as well as to people who have HIV.
  • Apply the facts about HIV to their own personal behavior.

Instructors trained in the program are able to meet the needs of diverse community groups. The program offers ways for instructors to:

  • Assess group needs.
  • Plan prevention education sessions.
  • Share HIV/AIDS information in factually accurate, nonjudgmental, and culturally sensitive ways.
  • Facilitate interactive sessions using educational tools such as posters, videos, letters, and brochures.
  • Make referrals to other HIV/AIDS resources in the community.

To learn more about Basic HIV/AIDS Program: Fundamentals, contact us at 774-0375. The Red Cross also offers a variety of HIV/AIDS related programs, for example:

  • HIV/AIDS Instructor Course
  • African-American HIV/AIDS Program
  • Hispanic HIV/AIDS Program
  • Workplace HIV/AIDS Program

HIV/AIDS is a serious killer, please don't hesitate to contact us for any information.
