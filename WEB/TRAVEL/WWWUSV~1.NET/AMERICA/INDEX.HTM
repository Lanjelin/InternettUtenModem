      The Foundation is headed by native-born U.S. Virgin Islanders -- world-ranked match racer and Olympic medallist, Peter W. Holmberg and businessman Michael A. Bornn.

    Peter W. Holmberg

      Holmberg was born in St. Thomas on October 4, 1960 and received his B.A. in Business Management from Sonoma State University, CA. Holmberg is the owner of Holmberg Enterprises (St. Thomas, U.S.V.I.) and divides his time between Holmberg Real Estate, Sobst ad Caribbean Sailmakers and his professional racing career. Holmberg is a Past Commodore of the St. Thomas Yacht Club and is Vice President of the Caribbean Yachting Association.

    Michael A. Bornn

      A U.S. Virgin island native, 38-year-old Bornn received his B.A. in Economics from Georgetown University and is a self-employed investments broker and dealer with Seslia Securities in St. Thomas. Bornn is the founder/director of the Johnny Harms Memorial: Virgin islands/Puerto Rico Billfish Tournament and retired director of the U.S.V.I. Open/Atlantic Blue Marlin Tournament, currently ranked as one of the world's top six sportfishing competitions.
