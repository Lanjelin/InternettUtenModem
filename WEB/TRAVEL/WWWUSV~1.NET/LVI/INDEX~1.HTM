   St. Croix, St. John and St. Thomas - each of our 3 beautiful VirginIslands is unique and offers you a different experience. What these Caribbean islands have in common is incomparable weather year-round, the politicalstability of the U.S. flag, IRS status as a tax-deductible site for businessmeetings, and extensive international shopping opportunities at duty-freeprices. The currency is the U.S. dollar and English is spoken with a Caribbean lilt. To people in the know, May, June, July, and August are the most beautifulmonths to be in the U.S. Virgin Islands. Crowdless, with balmy tropicalbreezes and crystal clear waters....

   We live in the Virgin Islands, one of Nature’s precious works ofart. Many who live here, and many others who visit, are uplifted, refreshedand renewed by simple things. A swim at Magens Bay, St. Thomas, Trunk Bay,St. John or Buck Island, off St. Croix, is no less an aesthetic experiencethan is the sight of a painting by native son, Camille Pissaro.

   Step into our home and let us show you around.....and oops almost forgot to tell you we drive on the left side ofthe road in the U.S. Virgins. Prior to 1917 the Danes owned the islandsand they - at the time - drove on the left. They changed but we never did.

Camille Pissarro Exhibit
December 16, 1996 through March 14, 1997

Location: Lilienfield house past the Synagogue on the opposite sideof the street.
  • This December, St. Thomas will become the center of the internationalart world's attention when recently recovered drawings and paintings ofartist Camille Pissarro premiere. The work of the world-renowned artistand founder of the French Impressionist movement will open on St. Thomasas part of the Bicentennial Celebration of the islands Hebrew Congregation.
  • The exhibition will showcase 50 of Pissarro's earliest drawings,oil sketches, sketchbook sheets and watercolors. The drawings are partof a collection from Olana, home of famous American landscape painter FredericChurch in Hudson, New York.
  • The exhibition entitled Camille Pissarro in the Caribbean, 1850-1855:Drawings from the Collection at Olana, will return some of Pissarro'sbest works to the island of St. Thomas where he received his early educationand, as a decendant of Marrano Jews, was a member of the island's Hebrew Congregation.
