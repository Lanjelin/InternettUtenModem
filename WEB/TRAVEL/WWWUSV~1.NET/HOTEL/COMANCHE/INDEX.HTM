Alexander Hamilton once lived within these hallowed halls.

Two hundred years later, Hollywood movie legends such as John Wayne, Maureen O'Hara and Laurence Olivier made regular visits. Today, European actors still visit the Comanche to rub shoulders with young American tourists, cosmopolitan South American businessmen and West Indian sailors.

The Club Comanche is a special, laid-back, almost magical place; easier to experience than to explain. Time seems to stand-still here. Ceiling fans whirl slowly. The air smells sweetly of hibiscus and mango and coconut. Parrots laugh. A smiling, white- jacketed waiter leisurely skips across a shady veranda. Palm trees seem to wave welcome. Brightly-painted fishing boats creak comfortably against a weathered dock. In the harbor, a frisky porpoise frolics. A fat moon chases a warm sun westward. Another perfect day evolves into yet another balmy, star-studded night.

The Club Comanche is a perfect reflection of the island of St. Croix. It is off-the-beaten path, yet thoroughly convenient. Friendly. Comfortable. Restful. Informal. Welcoming. It has a rich history and looks forward to a bright, sun-splashed future. It is famous for its quiet, restful tranquility and yet it is only a short walk to anywhere in historic Christiansted.
