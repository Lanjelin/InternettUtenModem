M/S IOFFE

Built in 1988 in Finland as a modern ice-breaking, ocean-going research vessel, M/S IOFFE was refitted in 1993 to enable it to carry passengers to Arctic regions.

M/S IOFFE has an international crew with a Canadian captain and is equipped with the latest navigation equipment.

When M/S IOFFE is not sailing in Greenlandic waters, it is employed in the Antarctic.

Facilities

40 double cabins/suites with portholes (except C-class cabins)
1 suite with single beds. En-suite toilet/shower
2 mini-suites with single beds. En-suite toilet/shower
19 A-class cabins, with single beds as standard. En-suite toilet/shower
14 B-class cabins, with single bed as standard. 2 cabins share toilet/shower.
4 C-class cabins with bunk beds. Shared toilet/shower in the corridor.
Restaurant with table service (93 guests)
Saloons Bar. Common room. TV lounge. Library. Kiosk.

Other facilites

Sauna. Small outdoor swimming pool. Medical and dental clinic.
