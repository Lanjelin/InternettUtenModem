Smilla Cabin Village
Ataa, Ilulissat

    
Smilla Cabin Village is named after the main character in Peter Høeg's famous novel "Miss Smillas feeling for snow", which was filmed by Bille August in the Spring of 1996. Some of the "Greenland" scenes were filmed in Ilulissat and the cabins which the film company used were subsequently sold to the "Smilla Cabin Village" project. These cabins have now been modernised and placed in Ataa, a disused settlement, which is situated on the idyllic Arveprinsens Ejland, approx. 70 km north of Ilulissat.

Smilla Cabin Village, Ataa


If you are really determined to get away from the busy daily stress and noise and enjoy the Greenlandic nature in all its magnificence and peace then here is your chance! Ilulissat Tourist Service in partnership with Tourist Nature offer you a visit to Smilla Cabin Village where you have a unique opportunity to enjoy the total experience of the Greenlandic nature in safety and comfort. It is possible to travel to Smilla Cabin Village by boat or by helicopter so you can combine a stay here with other excursions from Ilulissat or you may chose to spend your entire visit to Disko Bay at this incredibly beautiful place.

Boat trips
A passenger boat is permanently stationed at Smilla Cabin Village and it can be used for half or whole day trips around this wonderful area. The idyllically beautiful Eqi is only 2 hours from the cabin village and North Greenland's biggest bird cliff is within a day's sailing distance. There is also a rubber dinghy available to sail you from the cabin village out to Ataa Sund on a seal and iceberg safari.

Walking tours.
The area around Smilla Cabin Village offers great opportunities for short or long walks. Experienced mountain guides will be available to accompany you or you can walk alone. For people who are interested in flowers, birds or minerals the Ataa area offers many exciting experiences.

Angling
It will be possible to borrow fishing rods in Smilla Cabin Village so you can attempt to achieve every angler's dream - to catch the unbelievably tasty Greenlandic Trout in one of the many lakes.

Kayaking
Smilla Cabin Village also has several 2 person kayaks in which you can paddle out on the completely calm water of Tasersuaq or Ataa Sound. A kayak trip between the icebergs is certainly a worthwhile experience. The experienced kayak guide at the cabin village can advise you, or can accompany you on your trip.

Windsurfing and water skiing
Windsurfing and water skiing facilities are available in absolutely safe conditions on Tasersuaq, a large lake which is close to the cabin village.






Helicopter trip to Smilla Cabin Village

Helicopter trip to Smilla Cabin VillageWe fly by helicopter over some of the most beautiful scenery in Greenland to the wonderfully situated cabin village "Smilla".
After arrival we are accommodated in one of the cabins and the local guide informs you about the activities and excursions which are available in and from Smilla Cabin Village during our stay.
The number of days you spend in the cabin village is up to you to decide because you simply add the activities that you want and make up your own programme.
Days pass quickly in the unspoilt area of natural beauty around Smilla Cabin village. The place provides lots of different possibilities and there is something for every taste.
You can walk, look at the birds, stones and flowers. The tasty trout in the crystal clear lakes provide a true paradise for the angler. There is water-skiing, windsurfing and kayaking for the active visitor.
Regular boat excursions to various places of interest are arranged including Northern Greenland's biggest bird-cliff and the wonderfully beautiful Eqi, from where you can walk up onto the inland ice cap.
After your stay the helicopter collects us and takes us back to Ilulissat.

Boat trip to Smilla Cabin Village

On our journey through the scenically beautiful northern part of the Disko Bay we pass the beautiful little settlement of Oqaattut/Rodebay. This typical Greenlandic fishing and sealing village consists of colourful timber houses which picturesquely contrast with the wild nature which surrounds the village. We make a stop at Oqaattut to give you the opportunity of experiencing the genuine Greenlandic village atmosphere.
From here we continue towards the north into Ataa Sound.
With Arveprinsens Ejland on one side and the mainland on the other the narrow iceberg-filled sound offers magnificent scenery. There is ample opportunity to see some of the many seals which live in this area. After a wonderful long sail we arrive at Smilla Cabin Village in the late afternoon and one of the permanently stationed guides will welcome you.
The stay at Smilla Cabin Village, the food and activities are extras so you have the advantage of freely deciding how many days you wish to stay here.
You will be accommodated in one of the cabins and can freely choose to participate in the activities and excursions which are available.
