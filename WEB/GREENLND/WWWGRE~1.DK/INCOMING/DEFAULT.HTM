Getting to and around in Greenland

Greenland is located mid-way between Copenhagen/Denmark and New York/USA. We have scheduled flight services from Europe and North America to Greenland as follows:

Scandinavian Airlines System (SAS), from Copenhagen to Kangerlussuaq v.v.
6 flights per week in the summertime
3 flights per week in the wintertime

Firstair, from Ottawa to Kangerlussuaq v.v.
2 flights per week all year round

Greenlandair, from Keflavik to Kangerlussuaq and Nuuk
2 flight per week all year round

Greenlandair, local traffic
Several daily flights between Nuuk, Kangerlussuaq, Ilulissat and other destinations, except sundays.

Practical information

When travelling to Greenland, you should dressweise be prepare to sudden weather changes. In the summertime we will suggest you pack shorts, t-shirt, normal casual dress, warm jumper, warm overcoat, light raincoat, normal shoes, good walking booths, gloves and hat.

In the wintertime you may leave the shorts at home and pack warm winter clothes beside the above mentioned.

The currency is DKK (Danish Kroner)

Passport, generally your passport must be valid af least 6 months after your departure from Greenland.

Visa, when we know the nationality for all participants, we will check if visa is required. Visa is generally not needed for citizens from the "Western Europe" and "North America".
