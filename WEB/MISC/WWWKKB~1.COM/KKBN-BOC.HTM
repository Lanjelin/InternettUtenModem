Selected Books on Buddhism and Karma Kagyu Tradition in English

In alphabetical order

    Gampopa
    Gems of Dharma
    The Jewel Ornament of Liberation (tib. Dhagpo Thargyen) is the fundamental Kagyu text. We recommend this English edition.
    Altea Publ./Scotland, US$ 15.00

    Gampopa
    Precious Garland of the Sublime Path
    "Lamchog Rinchen Trengwa" - a short but important work by Gampopa.
    Rangjung Yeshe Publ. (Kathmandu), US$ 10.00

    Jamgon Kongtrul Rinpoche
    The Torch of Certainty
    Traditional teachings on the preliminary practices of the Kagyu diamond way of buddhism.
    Shambhala Publ. (Boston), US$ 12.00

    Karma Thinley
    The History of the Sixteen Karmapas of Tibet
    Detailed life stories of the Karmapas.

    Kunzig Shamar Rinpoche
    Change of Expression
    A commentary to Namshe Yeshe and Mahamudra explanations.
    Edition Dzambala (Dhagpo Kagyu Ling), US$ 15.00

    Lobsang P. Lhalungpa
    The Life of Milarepa
    The classic biography of the eleventh-century yogin and poet – the most beloved story of the Tibetan people and one of the most important life stories of the Kagyu lineage.
    Arkana Paperbacks / Penguin (New York), US$ 13.95

    Lama Ole Nydahl
    Entering the Diamond Way - My Path Among the Lamas
    Here is a genuinely compelling story, and spiritual odyssey, of Ole and Hannah Nydahl, who in 1968 became the first Western students of the great Tibetan master, the 16th Gyalwa Karmapa. After being recognized and trained by some of the highest lamas of the Kagyu lineage, Ole now transmits the blessing of the lineage and travels, as authorized lama, teaching and establishing centers of Tibetan Buddhism. He has so far established more than 180 Karma Kagyu Buddhist centers around the world. Hannah is a much sought-after translator and adviser to the highest Kagyu lamas.
    Blue Dolphin Publishing (Nevada City), US$ 12.95

    Lama Ole Nydahl
    Mahamudra – Boundless Joy and Freedom
    A commentary on the wishing prayer for the attainment of the ultimate Mahamudra given by His Holiness Rangjung Dorje (the third Karmapa, 1284 - 1339). Ole´s fresh commentary shows us the essence of Karmapa´s text: that our true essence is clear and limitless space.
    Blue Dolphin Publishing (Nevada City), US$ 9.95

    Lama Ole Nydahl
    Ngondro – The Four Foundational Practices of Tibetan Buddhism
    An excellent guide for the practice of the preliminaries of the Kagyu tradition (Ngondro). In a direct and very practical way, these practices are methods which allow us, most efficiently, to purify negativity and accumulate merit. Ole´s fresh and joyful teachings grant the right motivation for performing these effective practices.
    Blue Dolphin Publishing (Nevada City), US$ 9.95

    Lama Ole Nydahl
    Riding the Tiger
    After their years of studying and practice in the Himalayas, Hannah and Ole Nydahl were authorized to teach and start centers in the name of Karmapa. Work in the West starts with an audience with the Queen of Denmark. A basement in historical Copenhagen became the first Tibetan Buddhist center. The Karmapa's visit in 1974 then opens up the world... "Riding the Tiger" is the inside story of the development of Tibetan Buddhism in the West. In his refreshingly unsentimental style, Lama Ole shows all aspects of his work.
    Blue Dolphin Publishing (Nevada City), US$ 17.95

    Lama Ole Nydahl
    Teachings on the Nature of Mind
    A basic introduction to buddhism for »the intelligent and idealistic West«. It introduces the essential teachings of the Buddha. From this groundwork comes the Great Way, the unity and integration of compassion and wisdom in daily life, and the Diamond Way.
    Blue Dolphin Publishing (Nevada City), US$ 5.00

    Nalanda Translation Committee
    The Life of Marpa the Translator
    One of the most famous spiritual texts of Tibet: Marpa´s exiting life. Marpa established the Kagyu lineage in Tibet and was trained by Naropa.
    Shambhala Publ. (London), US$ 16.00

    Nik Douglas & Meryl White
    Karmapa - The Black Hat Lama of Tibet
    This book presents the lifestories of the 16 Karmapas, the "Black Hat Lamas" of Tibet. Historical sources and texts gave the basis for this collection of more than 800 years of spiritual tradition in Tibet. Includes a lot of information about the Kagyu lineage, the lineage treasures kept in Rumtek etc. Now one of the most important Kagyu books, because it has been written before the 16th Karmapa´s death in 1981.
    LUZAC & COMPANY Ltd. (London), US$ 20.00

    Chogyam Trungpa
    Born in Tibet
    Trungpa's autobiography: Account of how he was raised as a tulku in Tibet and how he fled to India to escape the Chinese.
    Shambhala Publ. (Boston), US$ 15.00

    Tsele Natsok Rangdol
    The Lamp of Mahamudra
    Tsele Natsog Rangdrol has been a widely known meditation master of the 17th century Nyingma- and Kagyu-schools. His text on basis, path and fruit of Mahamudra explains all main aspects of these teachings: Shine and Lagthong, the four yogas, five paths and ten bhumis etc..
    Rangjung Yeshe Publ. (Kathmandu), US$ 14.00

    Tsele Natsok Rangdol
    The Mirror of Mindfulness
    The four bardos are explained by one of the great Kagyu- and Nyingma masters of the 17th century.
    Rangjung Yeshe Publ. (Kathmandu), US$ 14.00

    Yeshe Drönma
    The Kunzig Shamar Rinpoches of Tibet
    Short life-stories of the Kunzig Shamarpas; after the Karmapa the most important lineage holders.
    printed in Hongkong, US$ 10.00
