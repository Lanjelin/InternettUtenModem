Despite its heroic, go-anywhere, do-anything demeanor, the Defender 90 is not uncivilized. For those who seek the rugged tenacity of the Defender Soft-Top but desire the weather protection, security and convenience of a fixed roof, the Defender 90 Station Wagon is also available.

Not everyone who buys a Defender immediately heads for the Serengeti or the jungles of Borneo (although some do). Some of Land Rover's best customers will, in fact, appreciate the security and all-weather protection of the Defender 90 Station Wagon, with its factory-installed aluminum top.

In addition to full-height doors and roll-up windows for the driver and front passenger, the Station Wagon features sliding rear windows, a pop-up sunroof and "Alpine" windows overhead that create a feeling of interior airiness and spaciousness. And, for added comfort, twill-effect upholstery and full carpeting are standard equipment.

Otherwise, the Station Wagon is every inch a Defender, complete with aluminum body, Safari Cage, boxed-steel frame, resilient coil-spring suspension, new aluminum 4.0-liter V-8 engine and rugged four-speed automatic transmission with permanent four-wheel drive. Like all Defenders, it features a full 9 inches of ground clearance under the differentials, it can ford water up to 20 inches deep and carry more than a ton of cargo almost any place a person can walk.

And like the Soft-Top, the Station Wagon can be personalized with literally dozens of items of Land Rover Vehicle Kit -- everything from safari equipment to bicycle racks to a CD changer. Defender 90s are truly as individual as the people who buy them.
