Toolkits

The best words I've found on this subject came from jonm@duncan.intel.com in response to my inquiry to wetleather@mom.isc-br.com:

"Each scoots tool kit will be somewhat different, although many items will crossover. The contents of the toolkit are determined by asking the question: "What repair jobs can I do on the road and what tools are needed?" An easy way to do this is to use the toolkit for the regular work/maintenance on the bike and if you need a special tool add it to the kit."
The following is a list I compiled mostly from Wetleather posts:

Tools:

  • Duct tape (a small roll)
  • crescent wrench
  • screwdriver with various phillips and flat blade bits in handle
  • maglight
  • For the tires:
    - tire pressure gauge
    - patch/plug kit or "Fit-a-Flat"
    - one of the tire valve stem caps should be the type that will remove/seat the valve stem
  • 3/8" socket driver
  • all 3/8" sockets that fit the bike (including the rear axle)
  • all 3/8" drive allen bits that fit the bike
  • compact set of allen wrenches (Craftsman)
  • credit card-sized voltmeter
  • A SOG/Leatherman or
    - razor knife
    - jeweler's screwdrivers
    - needlenose pliers

Parts and Pieces:

  • extra sparkplugs (gaped)
  • H-D "Emergency Belt" (for belt drive models)
  • 3' hunk of "zip-cord"
  • Fuses
  • extra headlight bulb
  • shop rag
  • zip-ties
  • safety wire
  • a length of fuel line
  • a couple of small hose claps
  • misc. nuts bolts washers (flat and lock)
  • locktite

For You:

  • First Aid Kit
