    RSSBUELL@gnn.com writes:
    I currently race a Buell, which had a CV Carb. Nothing but problems! Dead spots, Roll the throttle slow. Even spent $400.00 to have it tuned. No help!!!!

    I went to see Red Edmundson at QuickSilver and his crew installed the QuickSilver II 40MM. I am impressed, this Carb works. It has no accelerator pump and one jet. The Carb is slid action and works off vacum. When air crosses the jet, fuel is mixed in. With the air filter off you can see nothing but a mist in the mouth of the Carb when you hit the throttle.

    I am currently using a K&N 3 inch air filter for max air intake. The Carb comes with several dif jets, air filter and new cables. You can also install an aftermarket airfilter if you like. Thunder Alley December 1995 issue has a article on installation which is basically simple. As far as rejetting: I compete at Willow Springs, Ca., Las Vegas, NV, and Phonix and have not had to change the jet. Overall the performance increase was magnificient.

Bill Burton (bburton@us.DHL.COM), ASSHOLE #27 and a keeper of the R.M.H FAQ, adds:

"I have had my suspension mods on fer a coupla weeks now and wanted to report on them. I went to Works Fork Springs in the front with 1/2" over spacer. These are actually made by Progressive FWIW.

In tha rear I installed Works Street Shockers. These are alum bodied shocks with two black springs which provide a progressive rate. They have fully adjustable dampning and preload. I went 3/4 over in tha rear. These are custom built for each order with the riders weight (225 lbs) and riding style (aggressive) used as variables in tha manf. process.

Also, mine is the first Dyna with these shocks so my Poser value just shot through tha roof!

These are some mahvelous components. The Supersickle is very confidence inspiring and, while I have scraped tha pegs, I have not slammed the frame as I did three times w/stock suspension. I have jumped (small) the sled twice now, something I would have never attempted stock.

There is a section of road in tha Oakland Hills out here that consists of 5, 10, and 15 mph turns seperated by small straights. This road is now fun instead of scary. Leaned over hitting tha gas tha Supersickle fishtails telling me that I don't need much more power for my application."
