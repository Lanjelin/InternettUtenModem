The LEGO Group is establishing a new department in Billund, Denmark with the aim of developing digital key technologies based on the world's best toy concept, including a LEGO 3D database containing every LEGO element. We plan to develop and market the finest software in the world for kids.

To accomplish this aim, we are looking to build a highlyqualified, international staff, who want to influence the way kids of the future will be playing with LEGO products. Apart from fluency in English, you should have a high degree of enthusiasm and dedication to kids, technology, and to the LEGO concept. You are self-motivated and yet a good team player. And you are one of the best in your field.

We offer a challenging and creative playground with all the necessary hardware from expresso machines to SGI ONYX super computers - teaming up with ingenious nerds and nerdesses, 3D meta-artists, LEGO designers, pixel sharks, technical wizards and other earthly visionaries.

Candidates must have: excellent interpersonal and visual communication skills, professional experience of Alias, 3D Studio and Photoshop, keen interest in the digital marketplace and should be: very quality oriented, experienced professionals from a computer graphics environment, highly motivated self starters, team players, able to work under pressure to deadline, wired and on-line oriented, interested in and preferably active software gamers, keenly interested in contemporary visual and entertainment culture - both digital and traditional. For the development of a digital database consisting of all LEGO elements, we are looking for: Alias Modellers, Alias Figure Modellers, Virtual Reality Programmer, Database Programmers and Graphic Artists.

Alias Modeler

With the aim of establishing LEGO's database of digital bricks, the main task is to model LEGO bricks in different levels of detail (LOD). The Modeler possesses a solid knowledge of Alias, but is willing to learn new 3-D tools. The Modeler is skilled in both NURBS and polygons, and knows how to optimize a model for speed. The computer environment consists of SGI workstations and high-end computers.

Applicants with at least one year experience will be prioritized. Knowledge of Unigraphics or Imageware Surfacer would be an asset. Job # 970126

 

Alias Figure Modeler

With the aim of establishing LEGO's database of digital bricks, the main task is to model LEGO figure bricks in different levels of detail (LOD). The Modeler possesses a solid knowledge of Alias, but is willing to learn new 3-D tools. The Modeler is skilled in both NURBS and polygons, and knows how to optimize a model for speed. Emphasis will be placed on modeling dolls, clothing, and other plastic creatures. The computer environment consists of SGI workstations and high-end computers, and a 3-D laser scanner from KREON.

Applicants with at least one year experience will be prioritized. Knowledge of Unigraphics or Imageware Surfacer would be an asset. Job # 970127

 

Virtual Reality Programmer

With the aim of establishing LEGO's database of digital bricks, the main task is to create intelligent elements which will be assembled in a VR system to build digital LEGO models. You will be in charge of building the system, using an appropriate combination of different VR software. Your job will be to provide the assemblers with intelligent elements, and put on behaviors like weight, sound, collision detection and snap. You must be used to working in teams, know about the latest within VR, and be able to teach people to use VR and intelligent bricks. You have experience in making levels of detail (LOD). You are skilled in C and C++. You must have experience in Multigen Smartscene, Division or another high-end VR system. Job # 970128

 

Graphic Artist

With the aim of establishing LEGO's database of digital bricks, the main task is to augment the 3-D geometries with 2-D effects. You will be:

- Modifying and attaching artwork on digital LEGO bricks.
- Modify and attaching artwork on digital LEGO boxes.
- Creating and attaching texture maps to 3-D geometries.
- Scanning assembly instructions and other artwork.
- Working together with other departments who handle or create graphics.

You must be experienced in Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator, and Alias PowerAnimator experience would be a definite asset. You must be experienced with Macintoshes, and Unix experience would be an asset. Job # 970129

 

Database Programmers

This is a technical position involving the design and development of an objectoriented relational database which will support LEGO design, marketing and research activities in the entire LEGO Group. Required experience: C++ fluency, RDMS programming, UNIX expertise. Experience with Illustra is an absolute plus. Job # 960319
