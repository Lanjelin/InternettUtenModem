Space-travel game on the Internet

- the latest attraction on http://www.LEGO.com

The LEGO Pirate game which was launched on the Internet in October quickly became a very popular port of call. More than 50,000 people tried out the game during the first month.

Now yet another advertainment game (entertainment + advertisement) for children (and adults!) is being launched. This time it is a LEGO Exploriens game, in which players take part in an exciting space expedition. Language options for the game are English, German, Danish and Spanish. The new game may be a little difficult for small children, who will probably need some help along the way.

In the LEGO Exploriens game, players can communicate direct with other players on the Internet - so they can help each other solve the problems they encounter in the spacecraft.

And an Advent calendar...
Since 1st December, surfers have also been able to enjoy a LEGO Internet Advent calendar. It is not the usual kind, with pictures behind little doors that can be opened before the appointed day for a "sneak preview" of the seasonal pictures they conceal. The doors are programmed so that they cannot be opened before the right date (Danish meantime plus 12 hours) and, instead of a picture, each door reveals a new problem to be solved - and all participants have the chance to win LEGO sets and a visit to LEGOLAND Billund or LEGOLAND Windsor.

The calendar is easy to find. Just type http://www.LEGO.com and click on Santa Claus. Further information: Mads Nipper, Marketing Europe, LEGO System A/S, tel. +45 75 33 11 88
