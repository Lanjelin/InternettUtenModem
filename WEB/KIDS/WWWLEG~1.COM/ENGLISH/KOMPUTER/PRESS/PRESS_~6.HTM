GENERATION LEGO

JOB ADVERTISEMENT!

The LEGO Group has established a new department in Billund, Denmark with the aim of developing digital key technologies based on the world's best toy concept, including a LEGO 3D database containing every LEGO element. We plan to develop and market the finest software in the world for kids.

To accomplish this aim, we are looking to build a highly qualified, international staff, who want to influence the way kids of the future will be playing with LEGO products. Apart from fluency in English, you should have a high degree of enthusiasm and dedication to kids, technology, and to the LEGO concept. You are self motivated and yet a good team player. And you are one of the best in your field.

We offer a challenging and creative play ground with all the necessary hardware from expressomachines to SGI ONYX super computers - teaming up with ingenious nerds and nerdesses, 3D meta artists, LEGO designers, pixel sharks, technical wizards and other earthly visionaries. And yes, bring along a sense of humour!

System Administrator

Responsibilities include management of a large, state-of-the-art heterogenous computing environment, together with management of the high performance multiple net structure supporting that environment. Support of e-mail, WWW, netnews, and other software structures. Management of the interface to the rest of the computing structure of the database development/operations computing environments. Required experience: Expertise with UNIX and C programming, at least one year's experience with C++ programming and preferably knowledge of Relational Database. Job #: 960154

Relational/OO Database Designers

The LEGO Group is looking for database designers to design and develop an object-oriented relational database to support LEGO research & development, design, marketing, and other computer graphics activities. The database designers are responsible for designing and developing the database server, and will also design and develop the client software technology on a variety of hosls, including Macintosh, Unix, and Windows. Required experience: Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science, RDBMS programmering, Unix expertise, and proven experience of OO design utilising either C++ or small talk.
Experience with Illustra is a definite plus. Job #: 960380
